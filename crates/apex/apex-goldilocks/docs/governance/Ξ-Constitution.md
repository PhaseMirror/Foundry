## Article IX: Amendments

**9.1. Proposal.** Amendments to this Constitution may be proposed by any member of the Multiplicity Foundation. Proposals must be submitted as a pull request to this document and must include a detailed rationale for the proposed change.

**9.2. Quorum.** For an amendment to be considered for a vote, a quorum of 2/3 of the active repository contributors from the preceding 90 days must be present in the discussion of the proposal.

**9.3. Veto Threshold.** A proposed amendment is rejected if it is vetoed by 1/3 or more of the voting members. The veto period shall be 14 days from the time the quorum is met.

**9.4. Rollback Trigger.** An amendment may be rolled back within 30 days of its ratification if a critical flaw is discovered. The rollback procedure follows the same proposal, quorum, and veto thresholds as a new amendment.

**9.5. Kill-Switch.** In the event of a critical security vulnerability or existential threat, a 3/5 majority of the Multiplicity Foundation council can activate a temporary kill-switch, pausing all system-level changes until the threat is mitigated.
