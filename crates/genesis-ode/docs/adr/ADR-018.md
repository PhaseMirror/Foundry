# ADR-018: Phase 3 – Formalizing the Kingdom of Ends Test

**Status**: Proposed

**Context**
- The third formulation of Kant’s Categorical Imperative (Kingdom of Ends) requires that maxims be adoptable by all rational agents as law‑making members of a moral community.
- An algorithmic checklist was drafted:
  1. Isolate the maxim.
  2. Envision a legislating congress of rational agents adopting the maxim as a universal law.
  3. Verify that the law does not treat any rational agent merely as a means.
  4. Verify systemic coherence of the resulting law‑system.
  5. Verdict: perfect‑duty violation if any step fails.
- The checklist was applied to the lying‑to‑the‑murderer maxim, producing a perfect‑duty prohibition.

**Decision**
- Adopt the **Kingdom‑of‑Ends checklist** as a reusable procedural artifact for all Kantian ethical analyses in the project.
- Record the checklist in the ADR repository and reference it from any moral‑logic modules that evaluate maxims.

**Consequences**
- Provides a systematic way to ensure that proposed moral laws respect mutual autonomy and can be rationally legislated by all agents.
- Complements the Universal‑Law and Humanity checklists, delivering a full three‑pronged Kantian validation framework.
- Enables future extensions (e.g., integrating with AI alignment frameworks) to be grounded in a rigorously documented moral‑legislative process.
