# 2-Category Treatment of Recursion

**Version 1.0 – 2026-07-04**

## Positioning in the Stack

The 2-categorical treatment supplies the **higher-dimensional compositional structure** for recursion. It sits directly above Prime Monomial Matrices (PMat) and provides the native home for PIRTM Tensor-Recursive Kernels.

- **0-cells** = prime signatures (objects of the free compact-closed category or of PMat)
- **1-cells** = graded prime-monomial matrices / tensor operations / contractions (the actual computational content)
- **2-cells** = certified recursive rewrites / PIRTM unfoldings / natural transformations that witness lawful recursion steps

This turns recursion from a meta-level operation into a first-class 2-dimensional citizen of the theory. All lower-layer invariants (signature grading, prime-mass conservation via \(M\), prime-power stability, Phase Mirror duality, c-theorems) lift automatically to the 2-level.

## Working Definition

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be the (strict) symmetric monoidal category of prime signatures and prime-monomial matrices (PMat), equipped with its compact-closed structure.

A **2-category of recursive processes** \(\mathbf{Rec}(\mathcal{C})\) is defined as follows:

- **0-cells**: Objects of \(\mathcal{C}\) (i.e., signatures \(e \in \mathbf{Sig}\)).
- **1-cells** \(f : e \to f\): Morphisms of \(\mathcal{C}\) (prime-monomial matrices whose grading respects the signature difference).
- **2-cells** \(\alpha : f \Rightarrow g\): A 2-cell exists from \(f\) to \(g\) when there is a **lawful recursive step** (a PIRTM unfolding, a prime-gated recursion rule application, or a controlled contraction sequence) that transforms the 1-cell \(f\) into the 1-cell \(g\) while preserving:
  - Net signature (hence \(M(f) = M(g)\))
  - Prime-power purity (if started from pure prime powers)
  - CSC budgets and \(\Lambda_m\) priors
  - Phase Mirror duality (the 2-cell commutes with \(\Phi\))

Vertical composition of 2-cells corresponds to sequencing recursive steps. Horizontal composition corresponds to tensoring two independent recursive processes. The compact-closed structure lifts: cups, caps, and yanking extend to 2-cells in the evident way (a recursive process can be “bent” provided the grading condition holds).

In the strict setting, many coherence isomorphisms become identities; the only non-trivial data lives in the 2-cells that witness recursion.

## Key Proposed Theorems

| Theorem | Statement (high level) | Consequence |
|---------|------------------------|-------------|
| **2-Dimensional Prime-Mass Conservation** | Every 2-cell \(\alpha : f \Rightarrow g\) satisfies \(M(f) = M(g)\). | Prime mass is preserved at the level of recursive derivations, not just at the level of individual matrices. |
| **Prime-Power Stability at 2-Level** | If \(f\) is built from pure prime-power monomials, then any 2-cell reachable from \(f\) remains pure prime-power. | Recursive unfolding never introduces extraneous primes, even across multiple steps. |
| **Phase Mirror Lifts to 2-Cells** | The duality functor \(\Phi\) extends contravariantly to 2-cells: \(\Phi(\alpha) : \Phi(g) \Rightarrow \Phi(f)\). Dissonance is non-increasing along 2-cell paths. | Phase Mirror becomes a symmetry of the entire recursive 2-category; drift can be audited at the derivation level. |
| **Adelic c-Theorem at 2-Level** | Along any path of 2-cells (lawful recursive trajectory), the total adelic central charge \(C_A\) is non-increasing. On Bruhat-Tits trees the drop equals the total length of the minimal cuts crossed by the 2-cells. | The global second law holds for recursive derivations, not merely for static objects. |
| **Coherence for Recursive Diagrams** | All ways of composing and parenthesizing a sequence of recursive steps are connected by unique (or coherently isomorphic) 2-cells. | Recursive tensor networks have a well-defined “normal form” up to certified 2-dimensional rewiring. |

These theorems guarantee that every certified recursive plan generated by ACE remains sound when viewed as a 2-dimensional object.

## Connections to the Rest of the Program

- **PIRTM**: The recursion rules become the generating 2-cells. Different recursion strategies (prime-step, prime-branching, Goldilocks, etc.) correspond to different families of 2-cells with different monotonicity properties on \(c\).
- **CRMF**: Resonance predicates label certain 2-cells; activation of resonance is a horizontal or vertical composition step that remains inside the viability kernel.
- **Phase Mirror**: Acts as a contravariant 2-functor that reverses 2-cell direction while preserving dissonance decrease. Mirror-balanced recursive derivations are exactly those whose 2-cells are fixed (up to isomorphism) by \(\Phi\).
- **ACE / CSC Controller**: The controller now searches over 2-cell paths rather than mere 1-cell sequences. Each candidate path carries its own 2-dimensional certificate (the composite 2-cell) that can be checked for signature preservation, c-decrease, and budget compliance.
- **Diagrammatic Calculus**: String diagrams acquire recursive 2-cells. Yanking, sliding, and contraction remain sound; recursive boxes can be “opened” or “closed” via 2-cell rewrites without breaking prime-mass invariants.
- **Langlands**: Hecke correspondences and functoriality lift to 2-cells. A recursive step that applies a Hecke operator at a prime \(p\) corresponds to a 2-cell whose boundary L-functions differ by the expected algebraic factor.
