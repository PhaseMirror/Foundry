## 3. The Adelic c-Theorem Product Formula

**Theorem (Adelic c-Theorem Product Formula – Proposed)**

Let \( e_t \) be a trajectory generated by any lawful global flow (certified PIRTM recursion, CRMF resonance activations, Phase Mirror duality operations, or continuous gradient flows of the topological Phase Mirror) that respects the grading and prime-power stability conditions at every place.

Then the product of the local central charges (in exponential form) is **globally invariant**:

\[

\]

or, equivalently, the **total adelic central charge**

\[
C_A(e) := \sum_v c_v(e) \quad \text{(with appropriate normalization factors at } \infty\text{)}
\]

satisfies

\[
\frac{d}{dt} C_A(e_t) \leq 0
\]

with equality only at fixed points (adelic “CFTs”).

On the Bruhat-Tits realization, the drop in the finite-place contribution is exactly compensated by the change in the archimedean dissonance:

\[
\Delta c_p + \Delta c_\infty = 0 \quad \text{(or } \leq 0\text{ in the dissipative case)}

for each infinitesimal lawful step. This is the precise adelic strengthening of the classical c-theorem: local infrared decreases in central charge on p-adic trees are exactly balanced (or over-balanced) by archimedean stress-capacity adjustments.

**Proof sketch (high level)**

1. At finite places the p-adic c-theorem already gives monotonicity of each \( c_p \) separately (non-increasing along lawful tree flows).
2. The archimedean place contributes a continuous dissonance term whose gradient flow is constructed to be non-increasing in \( c_\infty \).

4. Phase Mirror duality (\( e \mapsto -e \)) acts as a reflection that swaps the sign of all local contributions while preserving the product, thereby enforcing the global balance.
5. Induction on recursion depth (finite places) + continuity of the archimedean flow closes the argument.
