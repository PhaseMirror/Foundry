## 1. Motivation

In the PETC framework we have a strict symmetric monoidal category with duals whose objects are prime signatures:

\[
\mathrm{Sig} \;\simeq\; \mathbb{Z}^{(P)} \qquad
(e \otimes f)_p = e_p + f_p, \quad
e^\vee = -e, \quad
I = 0.
\]

This category is already compact-closed in a degenerate (strict) sense: the cup and cap for any signature \(e\) are simply the identity on the unit object because \(e + (-e) = 0\) holds strictly.

The question arises: **what is the universal property of this construction?** In other words, is \((\mathrm{Sig}, \otimes, I, (-)^\vee)\) the *free* compact-closed category generated by the set \(P\) of primes (in the strict symmetric monoidal sense)?

An affirmative answer gives us:
- A precise universal property for extending interpretations (any assignment of primes to objects in another strict compact-closed category extends uniquely).
- A clean diagrammatic calculus whose rewrites are automatically certified by the multiplicity functor \(M\).
- A solid foundation for lifting to richer 2-categorical or recursive settings (PIRTM, Phase Mirror governance).

## 2. Definition of the Free Compact-Closed Category

Let \(G\) be a set (in our case \(G = P\), the set of primes).

The **free compact-closed category** \(\mathcal{F}(G)\) generated by \(G\) is the category with:

### Objects
The free abelian group on \(G\):

\[
\mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{F}(G)) = \mathbb{Z}^{(G)} = \{ e : G \to \mathbb{Z} \mid \operatorname{supp}(e) \text{ finite} \}.
\]

We write \(e = \sum_{g \in G} e_g \cdot g\) (formal linear combination with integer coefficients). Tensor is pointwise addition, unit is the zero function, dual is negation. This is exactly our \(\mathrm{Sig}\).

### Morphisms
Morphisms are generated by the following data, subject to the axioms of a strict symmetric monoidal category with duals (i.e., compact-closed axioms in the strict setting):

- **Structural isomorphisms** (all strict, hence identities after strictification):
  - Associators, unitors, braiding (symmetry) — all become identities because we strictify.
- **Duality generators** (cups and caps) for each generator \(g \in G\):
  \[
  \eta_g : I \to g^\vee \otimes g, \qquad
  \varepsilon_g : g \otimes g^\vee \to I.
  \]
  In the strict model these are simply \(\mathrm{id}_I\).
- **Naturality and coherence axioms** for the above (triangle identities, yanking/snake equations, naturality of braiding with respect to duality, etc.).

Because everything is strict, the only morphisms that survive are the identity morphisms on each object. In other words:

\[
\mathcal{F}(G) \;\simeq\; \mathbf{1}_{\mathrm{Sig}}
\]

(the discrete category on the set of signatures, with only identity arrows).

This is the **strict free compact-closed category** generated by \(G\).

## 3. Universal Property

**Theorem (Universal Property of Strict Free Compact-Closed Category).**

Let \(\mathcal{C}\) be any strict symmetric monoidal category equipped with a strict duality operation \((-)^\vee\) (i.e., a strict compact-closed category). Let \(f : G \to \mathrm{Ob}(\mathcal{C})\) be any function assigning an object of \(\mathcal{C}\) to each generator.

Then there exists a unique strict symmetric monoidal functor

\[
F : \mathcal{F}(G) \to \mathcal{C}
\]

such that \(F(g) = f(g)\) for all \(g \in G\), and \(F\) strictly preserves tensor, unit, and duals (hence also preserves cups and caps).

**Proof sketch.**  
On objects the functor is forced: it must send the formal sum \(\sum e_g \cdot g\) to the corresponding tensor product \(\bigotimes_g f(g)^{\otimes e_g}\) (with negative exponents interpreted via dual). On morphisms, strictness forces every structural isomorphism and every cup/cap to be sent to the corresponding identity or structural map in \(\mathcal{C}\). Uniqueness follows because every object and every (identity) morphism is generated from the data of \(G\) and the compact-closed operations.

**Corollary (for our multiplicity functor).**  
The multiplicity map \(M : \mathrm{Sig} \to \mathbb{Q}^\times\) (viewed as a functor from the discrete category on \(\mathrm{Sig}\) to the discrete category on \(\mathbb{Q}^\times\)) is the unique strict symmetric monoidal functor extending the assignment \(p \mapsto p \in \mathbb{Q}^\times\) (where each prime is sent to itself as a unit in the multiplicative group).

## 4. Diagrammatic Calculus (String Diagrams)

Because \(\mathcal{F}(G)\) is compact-closed, it admits a sound and complete diagrammatic language (string diagrams / Penrose notation):

- Each prime \(p\) with positive exponent \(+k\) is represented by \(k\) upward-pointing wires labeled \(p\).
- Negative exponent \(-k\) is represented by \(k\) downward-pointing wires labeled \(p\).
- Tensor product is horizontal juxtaposition (or vertical stacking, depending on convention).
- Contraction of a matched \(+1/-1\) pair on the same prime is connecting an up-wire to a down-wire.
- Cup \(\eta_p\) is a “U-bend” turning a down-wire into an up-wire (or creating a loop).
- Cap \(\varepsilon_p\) is a “∩-bend”.
- Yanking / snake equations allow straightening any zig-zag into a straight wire (or removing a closed loop).

**Key semantic fact (via \(M\)):**  
Any closed diagram (no free wires) whose net signature is the zero signature evaluates under \(M\) to the scalar \(1 \in \mathbb{Q}^\times\). In other words, every topologically valid rewiring that preserves the net exponent vector also preserves the multiplicity value. This is exactly the conservation certificate we need for PETC.

**Example – Yanking for a single prime \(p\)**

```
Down-wire          Up-wire
   p                 p
   |                 |
   \______cup_______/
          |
       (loop evaluates to 1 under M)
          |
   \______cap_______/
   |                 |
   p                 p
```

After yanking the diagram reduces to the straight identity wire on \(p\) (or to the empty diagram if fully closed). Both sides have net signature \(0\) for that prime and \(M = 1\).

## 6. Lifting to Richer Models (Non-Degenerate Morphisms)

If we want non-trivial morphisms (actual computational content), we can consider:

1. **Prime-monomial matrices**  
   Morphisms \(e \to f\) are matrices whose entries are monomials \(c \cdot \prod p^{k_p}\) where the exponent vector of the monomial exactly compensates the difference \(f - e\). Composition and tensor product preserve the exponent accounting. \(M\) still acts as a functor by taking the product of all monomial coefficients (ignoring the prime part, which cancels by construction).

2. **Free compact-closed category without strictification**  
   Keep the associators, unitors, and braidings as non-identity isomorphisms. Then there are many distinct morphisms between the same pair of objects that become equal only after applying a coherence theorem. \(M\) still factors through the quotient by coherence, sending every coherence isomorphism to the identity scalar.

3. **2-categorical enrichment (for recursion)**  
   Treat diagrams themselves as 1-morphisms and rewrites (or higher homotopies) as 2-morphisms. This opens the door to modeling PIRTM-style recursive tensor constructions as 2-cells that preserve the underlying signature.

Any of these richer models still admits \(M\) as a strong monoidal functor that “forgets wiring” and only sees net prime mass.

## 7. Implementation Notes

### Lean
- The discrete model is already essentially formalized (see previous ADR scaffold).
- Adding a `CompactClosedCategory` instance for `Discrete Sig` is mostly `rfl` (strictness).
- The universal property can be stated as an `Inductive` or via a `Free` construction; the main lemmas are that cups/caps are sent to `1` under `M`.
- For a non-strict version one can use `CategoryTheory.Monoidal.Free` from mathlib and then quotient by the abelian-group relations on objects.

### Python
Extend the existing `multiplicity.py` with a lightweight diagrammatic layer:

```python
from dataclasses import dataclass
from typing import List, Tuple

Wire = Tuple[int, int]  # (prime, direction)  +1 up, -1 down

@dataclass
class Diagram:
    wires: List[Wire]
    connections: List[Tuple[int, int]]  # pairs of wire indices that are connected

    def net_signature(self) -> Signature:
        sig: Signature = {}
        for p, d in self.wires:
            sig[p] = sig.get(p, 0) + d
        return sig

    def is_balanced(self) -> bool:
        return all(v == 0 for v in self.net_signature().values())

    def evaluate_M(self) -> Fraction:
        if not self.is_balanced():
            raise ValueError("Diagram not closed/balanced")
        return Fraction(1)  # or compute actual scalar if coefficients present
```

Hypothesis tests can generate random balanced diagrams and verify that `evaluate_M` always returns 1 and that rewrites (yanking, sliding, etc.) preserve the value.

## 8. Relation to Larger Program

- **PETC conservation certificates** become instances of “any two diagrams with the same net signature are equal after applying \(M\)”.
- **ACE contraction planning** can be viewed as searching for a sequence of diagrammatic rewrites (contractions) that reduces a large tensor network while preserving the global signature.
- **Phase Mirror governance** is literally the enforcement of duality: every diagram must remain balanced under the mirror operation (negation of all exponents).
- **PIRTM / tensor recursion** can be modeled by allowing 2-morphisms that duplicate or recurse sub-diagrams while preserving the underlying signature (the recursion must be “prime-gated”).
- **CRMF resonance** appears as additional relations or 2-cells that identify certain wirings when resonance conditions (matching prime exponents across channels) are met.

## 10. Conclusion

The free compact-closed category generated by the set of primes \(P\), strictified in the natural way, has as its underlying object set precisely our prime signatures \(\mathrm{Sig}\). Its universal property guarantees that any interpretation of the primes in another strict compact-closed category extends uniquely to a functor that automatically preserves all certified diagrammatic rewrites.

The multiplicity functor \(M\) is the canonical semantics that collapses every valid wiring to its net prime mass. This gives a mathematically rigorous justification for treating PETC signatures as a type system and Multiplicity Theory as its sound semantic interpretation — exactly the “encoding underneath, labeling on top” architecture we have been developing.

This layer completes the categorical foundation needed for the next stages of the program: certified diagrammatic tensor recursion, Phase-Mirror duality governance, and the integration of resonance-coupled (CRMF) and prime-indexed recursive tensor (PIRTM) constructions.
