# ADR-007: Lean 4 Formal Verification Plan for ZK Circuits & Smart Contracts

## Status
**Adopted**

## Context
The Universal Atomic Calculator (UAC) currently relies on Lean 4 (`SnapKitty`) to mathematically prove the physics bounds (MA-VQE energy constraints, ThermalWindow, HSEC entropy, and Q-SQD physical fingerprint stability) at the Rust and FPGA levels. However, the finality layer (the Circom ZK circuits in `circuits/` and the EVM Solidity contracts in `contracts/`) operates outside of this formal Lean verification envelope.

To achieve true end-to-end mathematical provenance under the Sedona Spine mandate, we must expand our Lean 4 formalization down to the ZK circuits and the smart contracts, ensuring the arithmetic constraints executed on-chain perfectly match the proven constraints generated upstream.

## Decision
We will implement a phased Lean 4 formalization plan targeting the most critical components of the finality layer.

### Phase 1: Circom Circuit Formalization (`circuits/`)
The primary goal is to prove that the rank-1 constraint systems (R1CS) generated by Circom faithfully represent the intended mathematical predicates without under-constrained variables or overflow vulnerabilities.
1. **`DriftBound.circom`**: Formalize the temporal drift constraint $\delta \le 0.3\xi$. Prove that the 80-bit `LessThan` comparator and scaling arithmetic ($10\delta \le 3\xi$) are cryptographically sound over the prime field scalar.
2. **`attestation.circom`**: Formalize the public/private signal mappings and verify that the output properly constrains the cryptographic commitments (nullifier derivation, digest injection).

### Phase 2: Solidity Contract Formalization (`contracts/`)
The goal is to prove the state machine invariants and execution logic of the EVM settlement layer.
1. **`AttestationRegistry.sol`**: Model the contract state transitions in Lean 4. Prove the replay-protection invariant (a nullifier can only transition from `false` to `true` once, and any subsequent attempt reverts).
2. **`ConsentRegistry.sol`**: Prove the revocation logic invariants, ensuring that once a consent commitment is flagged as revoked, it can never be legally consumed by the `AttestationRegistry`.
3. **ZK Verifier Bridges**: Formalize the interface guarantees between the `AttestationRegistry` and the `IGroth16Verifier`, proving that the 5-signal `attestPub` array is strictly bounded and cannot be maliciously mutated.

## Consequences
- **Positive:** Closes the final mathematical gap in the UAC pipeline. Yields a sorry-bounded formally verified path from MA-VQE pulse execution on the FPGA to decentralized finality on the EVM.
- **Negative:** Significant engineering effort required. Modeling EVM execution semantics and finite field R1CS constraints in Lean 4 is notoriously complex and slow.
