# ADR-107: Predictive Thermal Scheduler & Quantum Anomaly Detection

## Status
Proposed

## Axis (Phase Mirror tension class)
urgency vs capacity

## Owner (multi-agent lever)
`the-publisher`

## Dissonance Score
- Impact = severity (3) x blast radius (2) = **6**

- **Score = 3.0**  (Evolution plan rank 4 of 6)

## Context (stated intent vs implementation)
The current Isolation Forest governance is reactive — it flags anomalies after
they occur, and breaches end in an abrupt `QuantumM::Collapse`. The brief
proposes (a) an LSTM trained on Prometheus telemetry to predict thermal spikes
30–60 s ahead and preemptively shift low-priority sessions to `d=8`, and
(b) a Quantum Variational Circuit (VQC) for superior multi-dimensional anomaly
detection.

### Stated intent (documents)
- Brief "Deepen Operational Governance" — predictive thermal scheduler +
  quantum-enhanced anomaly detection; avoid abrupt collapse; preserve
  throughput.
- `observability/` exports Prometheus telemetry; governance currently uses a
  classical Isolation Forest.

### Implementation reality (observability/, crates/)
- Governance is reactive only; no predictive thermal model exists.
- No quantum circuit is in the anomaly pipeline; `QuantumM::Collapse` remains
  the breach response.

### Manifested boundary
Leaked (unmanifested): NO — net-new adaptive capability. Claimed
"self-healing / proactive" behavior must remain `Proposed` until the
preemptive shift preserves the 100-qudit boundary (Constraint 2) and is
reconstructible from telemetry + WORM (Constraint 4).

## Decision (the lever)
Layer predictive + quantum-enhanced governance on top of, not instead of, the
existing reactive baseline:

1. **Predictive thermal scheduler**: train an LSTM on historical Prometheus
   telemetry to forecast thermal load 30–60 s ahead; when a breach is
   predicted, shift low-priority sessions to `d=8` *before* the threshold,
   avoiding `QuantumM::Collapse`.
2. **Quantum anomaly detection**: add a parameterized VQC to the anomaly
   pipeline for superior clustering of normal telemetry; its flags augment (not
   replace) the Isolation Forest. The classical model remains the safe
   fallback.
3. **Safety envelope (formal)**: prove `D8ShiftPreservesBoundary` and
   `PreemptiveShiftMonotone` in Lean4 — any `d=8` shift must keep the active
   space within the 100-qudit CAS(20,20) envelope and must never increase
   collapse probability vs the reactive baseline.
4. **Auditability**: every preemptive shift and VQC flag is recorded with the
   predicting model version + telemetry window in the WORM ledger so the action
   is reconstructible (Constraint 4).

This makes governance proactive and self-healing while keeping the verified
baseline as a guaranteed fallback.

### Objective
Replace reactive anomaly detection with a predictive scheduler using LSTM and a
Quantum Variational Circuit (VQC) for more accurate, proactive anomaly
detection.

### Motivation
Current Isolation Forest is reactive; thermal spikes are only detected after
they occur. Predictive scheduler can shift sessions to `d=8` before breach,
preserving throughput.

### Non-Goals
- Do not replace existing Isolation Forest immediately; deploy as a sidecar and
  compare performance.
- Do not require quantum hardware; VQC is simulated for development.

### Technical Approach
1. **Predictive Scheduler**: Train an LSTM on historical telemetry (utilization,
   error rates, session counts) to forecast `util` 60 seconds ahead. If
   forecasted `util > 0.85`, preemptively throttle low‑priority sessions.
2. **Quantum Anomaly Detection**: Use a VQC (e.g., 4 qubits) to encode the 5D
   telemetry vector into a quantum state; measure expectation values as an
   anomaly score. Train via hybrid classical‑quantum optimization (PyTorch +
   Pennylane) on WORM data. If VQC score exceeds threshold, escalate to ALP.

### Blockchain Anchor (Invariant 5)
- **Problem**: `SIG_GOV_KILL` and anomaly flags are logged to
  Elasticsearch/WORM (file-based). If a malicious insider or cloud failure
  corrupts that WORM, the chain of custody breaks.
- **Implementation**: Introduce `GovernanceAnchor.sol`. Every hour, the
  NarrativeAuditor computes a Merkle root of the past hour's governance logs
  (AI anomaly scores, thermal shifts, ALP escalations). The sidecar submits
  this root to `GovernanceAnchor.sol`.
- **Result**: In a legal dispute about autonomous action (e.g., throttling a
  client unfairly), the exact state of the AI's "brain" at that moment is
  cryptographically anchored. The record of *why* the system acted is now
  forever immutable, not just the result.

### Implementation Phases
- **Week 1‑3**: Collect 72h of telemetry; train LSTM and VQC; evaluate
  precision/recall.
- **Week 4‑6**: Integrate LSTM predictor into FPGA orchestrator; add preemptive
  throttling logic.
- **Week 7‑9**: Deploy VQC sidecar as a replacement for Isolation Forest
  (initially in shadow mode, logging decisions without action).
- **Week 10‑12**: Gradual switch‑over; monitor metrics.

## Consequences
- **Positive**: avoids abrupt collapse; preserves throughput; superior
  multi-dimensional anomaly detection; reactive baseline retained as fallback.
- **Negative / Constraints**: model drift risk requires periodic retraining and
  monitoring; VQC needs a simulator/QC backend; false positives from prediction
  must not degrade SLA — bounded by the `PreemptiveShiftMonotone` proof.
- **Verification Strategy**: `D8ShiftPreservesBoundary` and
  `PreemptiveShiftMonotone` prove safety; a regression test simulates a thermal
  spike and confirms the scheduler shifts to `d=8` without boundary violation.

## Metrics (resolution is confirmed when)
- An LSTM scheduler exists with a telemetry-trained model and a `d=8`
  preemption path exercised by a test.
- A VQC anomaly detector is wired alongside the Isolation Forest with a
  governance test.
- `D8ShiftPreservesBoundary` / `PreemptiveShiftMonotone` are proved in `lean/`
  and the reactive fallback remains enabled.
- Precision/recall of VQC ≥ Isolation Forest (target false‑positive < 0.1%).
- LSTM reduces `QuantumM::Collapse` triggers by ≥50%.
