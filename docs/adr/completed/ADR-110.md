## Context (stated intent vs implementation)
The UAC's promise is "forever immutable" verification. Today, attestation is
per-run, governance logs live in file-based WORM/Elasticsearch, and proof
patches / AEGISS rationale / orchestrator decisions are local. None of these
operational states are cryptographically anchored to a public ledger, so the
"forever" guarantee covers results but not the *process and rationale* behind
them.

### Stated intent (documents)
- Brief "The New Blockchain Anchor Cross-Cutting Requirement" — add Invariant 5:
  every enhancement anchors its operational state to a Solidity contract
  periodically (≤ 1 hour).
- ADR-105 (STARK batching) supplies the gas subsidy that makes hourly anchoring
  economically trivial.
- ADR-106 (Dilithium) supplies the quantum-safe signature that keeps anchors
  verifiable for a century.

### Implementation reality (contracts/, scripts/)
- `contracts/AttestationRegistry.sol` exists but has no per-layer governance /
  orchestrator / rationale / proof anchor contracts.
- `scripts/submit_worm_attestation.sh` submits WORM events but no on-chain root.
- No aggregated daily anchor exists; state roots are not computed.

### Manifested boundary
Leaked (unmanifested): NO — net-new cross-cutting capability. Claimed
"forever immutable at scale" remains `Proposed` until anchors are provably
periodic (≤ 1 hour), verifiable, and funded by the ADR-105 gas reduction.

## Decision (the lever)
Institutionalize the Anchor Mandate (Invariant 5) via a single aggregation
layer:

1. **Per-layer anchor contracts**: each enhancement owns a dedicated contract —
   `ProofRegistry.sol` (ADR-104), `BatchAttestation.sol` (ADR-105/106),
   `GovernanceAnchor.sol` (ADR-107), `OrchestratorState.sol` (ADR-109),
   `ChemicalRationale.sol` (ADR-108).
2. **Periodic roots**: each layer computes a Merkle root of its rolling state
   (hourly for governance; every 10 blocks for orchestrator; per-patch for
   proofs; per-reduction for AEGISS) and submits it to its anchor contract.
3. **Aggregated daily anchor**: `AnchorRegistry.sol` accepts the daily
   aggregated STARK proof covering all layer roots; this is the single on-chain
   write that funds the whole mandate via ADR-105's gas savings.
4. **Forever verification**: off-chain payloads (Q‑SQD hashes, governance logs,
   DFT pre-screening, proof `Expr` hashes, resource digests) stay in
   IPFS/Arweave, bonded to the on-chain root via recursive verification. Target:
   total daily anchor cost < ~200k gas (≈ < $5).
5. **Quantum-safe**: all anchor submissions are Dilithium-signed (ADR-106) so
   the 2026 anchors remain verifiable in 2040+.

This welds every operational layer (AI, FPGA, formal proofs, chemistry) to the
EVM: "immutable" becomes mathematically enforced, cryptographically locked,
and legally impenetrable.

### Anchor Structures
- `GovernanceRoot`: Merkle root of {AI anomaly scores, thermal shifts,
  ALP escalations, `SIG_GOV_KILL` events} for the hour.
- `OrchestratorRoot`: hash of {session dimensions, thermal allocations, schedule
  decisions} at the 10-block snapshot.
- `AEGISSRoot`: hash of {molecule spec, DFT entropy analysis, selected
  CAS(20,20) space, linked FeMoco sim} per reduction.
- `ProofRoot`: SHA‑256 of each newly generated Lean `Expr` + commit timestamp.
- `BatchRoot`: Merkle root of a day's Q‑SQD attestation hashes.

### Schedule
- UTC 00:00 daily aggregated anchor (all roots folded into one STARK proof).
- Intra-day roots: hourly (governance), every 10 blocks (orchestrator),
  event-driven (proofs, AEGISS, batch windows).

### AnchorRegistry.sol logic
- `submitAggregate(root, starkProof)`: verifies the aggregated STARK, stores
  `root` + `block.timestamp`, emits `AnchorFinalized`.
- `verifyLayer(rootId, merkleProof, leaf)`: off-chain callers prove a specific
  layer leaf binds to the stored daily root.
- Access-controlled writer (Dilithium-verified sidecar only).

## Consequences
- **Positive**: "forever immutable" now covers process + rationale, not just
  results; disputes about autonomous action / SLA / methodology are
  cryptographically resolvable; cost trivial via ADR-105 subsidy.
- **Negative / Constraints**: additional on-chain surface to audit; dependency
  on ADR-105 (batching) and ADR-106 (Dilithium) landing first; off-chain
  availability (IPFS/Arweave) required for full verification.
- **Verification Strategy**: `AnchorPeriodic` and `AggregateBindsAllLayers`
  prove roots are submitted within the period and that the daily aggregate
  commits every layer root; a governance test submits mock roots and verifies
  reconstruction.

## Risk
- **Risk**: Off-chain payload loss (IPFS/Arweave) breaks full verification even
  if the on-chain root is intact.
- **Impact**: Medium.
- **Mitigation**: Pin + replicate off-chain payloads; store content hashes in
  the WORM ledger as a second bond; alert on availability regression.
