\begin{abstract}
We formalize the \emph{MultiplicityCell} as a finite-dimensional surrogate of the universal multiplicity recursion on the lawful Hilbert space $H_{\mathrm{lawful}}$.
The cell realizes the operator
\[
  \psi_{t+1} = P_E\bigl(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}\,T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi_t, x_t)\bigr),
\]
with $T_{\Lambda_m}$ a residual approximation of the universal spectral operator $U = A + B + E$.
We specify the prime block $A$, time-sieve block $B$, internal block $E$, the constitutional projector $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$, and the ethical projector $P_E$ in a finite-dimensional setting, and relate their Lipschitz properties to an empirical ACE (Absolute Contraction Energy) budget via the Banach fixed-point theorem.\footnote{For background on spectral normalization and Lipschitz control in neural nets, see e.g.\ Miyato et al.\ and subsequent expositions on spectral normalization and Lipschitz-bounded architectures.[web:89][web:104][web:107] For chaotic time-series benchmarks such as Mackey--Glass, see standard references and comparative studies.[web:85][web:99][web:101][web:102]} 
We then present a concrete validation protocol based on chaotic sequence prediction and channel-diversity analysis, together with an executable PyTorch implementation of the MultiplicityCell.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Executive Summary}

Multiplicity Theory models lawful dynamics on an ambient multiplicity Hilbert space
\[
  H = \ell^2(\mathbb{P}) \,\widehat{\otimes}\, L^2(\mathbb{R}) \,\widehat{\otimes}\, \mathbb{C}^d,
\]
with a universal spectral operator $U = A + B + E$ generating evolution on a constitutional subspace $H_{\mathrm{lawful}} \subset H$.[web:104]
The MultiplicityCell is a finite-dimensional surrogate of this recursion, suitable for neuromorphic learning experiments and numerical validation of the ACE budget and lawfulness constraints.

The core recursion is
\[
  \psi_{t+1} = P_E\Bigl(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}\bigl(\psi_t + \Lambda_m(A(\psi_t) + B(\psi_t) + E(\psi_t,x_t))\bigr)\Bigr),
\]
where:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item $A$ is a prime-channel mixing block (banded, spectrally constrained).
  \item $B$ is a time-sieve block (1D convolution over prime index, a discrete Fourier-multiplier surrogate).
  \item $E$ is an internal block (channel-wise gated MLP with spectral-radius control).
  \item $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ enforces constitutional lawfulness (norm cap + structured sparsity).
  \item $P_E$ enforces an ethical viability kernel (diversity-promoting reweighting on channel energies).
  \item $\Lambda_m$ is the multiplicity constant controlling overall contraction.
\end{itemize}

Under suitable spectral-norm and scaling constraints, the composite map
\[
  F = P_E \circ \Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}} \circ T_{\Lambda_m}
\]
acts as a contraction on a ball in the finite-dimensional state space, yielding a unique fixed point and stable recursive dynamics by Banach's fixed-point theorem.[web:105][web:108][web:111][web:113]
This gives an empirical ACE budget: increasing $\Lambda_m$ eventually destroys contractivity, leading to observable phase transitions in stability, prediction error, and prime-channel diversity.

We specify:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item A precise finite-dimensional state space and operator definitions.
  \item Lipschitz bounds relating spectral norms of $A,B,E$ to an ACE threshold.
  \item A PyTorch implementation of the MultiplicityCell.
  \item A validation protocol using the Mackey--Glass chaotic time series to probe stability and diversity across a sweep of $\Lambda_m$.[web:85][web:91][web:94][web:101]
\end{enumerate}

\section{Finite-Dimensional Surrogate of $H_{\mathrm{lawful}}$}

\subsection{State Space}

We consider a finite-dimensional state space
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f} \cong \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f},
\]
intended as a truncated approximation of $H_{\mathrm{lawful}} \subset H$.
Here:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item $n_p \in \mathbb{N}$ is the number of \emph{prime channels}, a finite truncation of the $\ell^2(\mathbb{P})$ factor.
  \item $n_f \in \mathbb{N}$ is the number of features per channel, a finite truncation of $L^2(\mathbb{R}) \otimes \mathbb{C}^d$.
\end{itemize}

We write $\psi \in \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ as a matrix $\psi = (\psi_{i,j})_{1 \le i \le n_p,\, 1 \le j \le n_f}$ and equip $\mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ with the Frobenius norm
\[
  \|\psi\|_F = \Bigl(\sum_{i=1}^{n_p}\sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \psi_{i,j}^2\Bigr)^{1/2}.
\]

We interpret the $i$-th row $\psi_{i,\cdot} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ as the feature vector associated to the $i$-th prime channel.

\subsection{Residual Surrogate of $U = A + B + E$}

\begin{definition}[Residual update]
Let $A,B,E : \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f} \to \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ be nonlinear maps with Lipschitz constants $L_A,L_B,L_E$ with respect to $\|\cdot\|_F$.
For $\Lambda_m > 0$, define
\[
  T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x) = \psi + \Lambda_m\bigl(A(\psi) + B(\psi) + E(\psi,x)\bigr),
\]
where $x$ is an external input.
\end{definition}

In the infinite-dimensional setting, $A,B,E$ represent the prime block, time-sieve block, and internal block of the universal spectral operator $U$, respectively.
In our finite surrogate, they are implemented by spectrally-controlled neural network layers, as in Section~\ref{sec:impl}.[web:104][web:107][web:110]

\subsection{Constitutional and Ethical Projectors}

\begin{definition}[Constitutional projector $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$]
The constitutional projector is a map $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}} : \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f} \to \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ defined as the composition of:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item A norm cap on each row: for a parameter $R > 0$, each channel vector $v \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ is mapped to
  \[
    v \mapsto \frac{R}{\max(R,\|v\|_2)}\,v.
  \]
  \item A structured sparsity operator that zeros coordinates below a quantile threshold in each row, enforcing prime-like support patterns.
\end{enumerate}
This map is non-expansive with respect to $\|\cdot\|_F$:
\[
  \|\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(\psi) - \Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(\phi)\|_F \le \|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Ethical projector $P_E$]
Let $E \subset \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ be a nonempty closed subset interpreted as an ethical viability kernel.
An ethical projector is a (possibly approximate) firmly non-expansive map $P_E : \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f} \to E$ satisfying
\[
  \|P_E(\psi) - P_E(\phi)\|_F^2 \le \langle P_E(\psi) - P_E(\phi),\, \psi - \phi\rangle
\]
for all $\psi,\phi$, where the inner product is the Frobenius inner product.
\end{definition}

In our concrete implementation, $P_E$ acts on channel energies to promote diversity via a reweighting step, approximating a proximal map of an entropy-based functional.[web:104][web:110]

\subsection{Recursive Map and ACE Budget}

\begin{definition}[Multiplicity recursion]
Given maps $A,B,E$, projectors $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}},P_E$ as above, and $\Lambda_m > 0$, define
\[
  F(\psi,x) = P_E\bigl(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x))\bigr).
\]
For fixed input sequence $(x_t)_{t\ge 0}$, the Multiplicity recursion is
\[
  \psi_{t+1} = F(\psi_t,x_t).
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Lipschitz bound and contraction]\label{prop:lipschitz}
Assume that $A,B,E$ have Lipschitz constants $L_A,L_B,L_E$ in $\|\cdot\|_F$ and that $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ and $P_E$ are non-expansive.
Then for fixed $x$,
\[
  \|F(\psi,x) - F(\phi,x)\|_F \le \Bigl(1 + \Lambda_m(L_A+L_B+L_E)\Bigr)\,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
In particular, if $\Lambda_m$ is chosen so that
\[
  1 + \Lambda_m(L_A+L_B+L_E) < 1,
\]
then $F(\cdot,x)$ is a contraction in $\|\cdot\|_F$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
By the residual definition and Lipschitz bounds,
\[
  \|T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x) - T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x)\|_F 
  \le \|\psi - \phi\|_F + \Lambda_m(L_A+L_B+L_E)\,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
Since $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ and $P_E$ are non-expansive, applying them does not increase distances, hence the stated bound.
\end{proof}

In practice, we impose spectral-norm constraints on the linear operators inside $A,B,E$, using spectral normalization or related techniques, to control $L_A,L_B,L_E$.[web:89][web:104][web:107][web:110]

\begin{theorem}[Finite-dimensional ACE budget and fixed point]\label{thm:banach}
Suppose that for all inputs $x$ in a given set and for all $\psi$ in a closed ball $B_R = \{\psi : \|\psi\|_F \le R\}$, $F(\psi,x)$ lies in $B_R$ and the Lipschitz constant of $F(\cdot,x)$ satisfies
\[
  \mathrm{Lip}(F(\cdot,x)) \le q < 1.
\]
Then, for each fixed stationary input $x$, the recursion $\psi_{t+1} = F(\psi_t,x)$ admits a unique fixed point $\psi^\ast \in B_R$, and for any initial $\psi_0 \in B_R$, we have $\psi_t \to \psi^\ast$ at a geometric rate.[web:105][web:108][web:111][web:113]
\end{theorem}

This gives a finite-dimensional ACE budget: we regard the supremum of $\Lambda_m$ values for which the conditions of Theorem~\ref{thm:banach} hold as an empirical ACE threshold.

\section{Concrete Implementation in PyTorch}\label{sec:impl}

We now specify a concrete MultiplicityCell implementation that realizes the operators described above.

\subsection{Architecture}

Let $n_p$ be the number of prime channels and $n_f$ the number of features per channel.
The cell receives a state $\psi_t \in \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f}$ and an input $x_t \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ and returns $\psi_{t+1}$.

\paragraph{Prime block $A$.}
We flatten $\psi$ to a vector in $\mathbb{R}^{n_p n_f}$, apply a linear map $W_A$ with spectral normalization, followed by a smooth nonlinearity, and reshape back:
\[
  A(\psi) = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl(\sigma(W_A\,\mathrm{vec}(\psi))\bigr),
\]
where $\sigma$ is e.g.\ GELU or $\tanh$, and $W_A$ is constrained so that $\|W_A\|_2$ is bounded.[web:89][web:104]

\paragraph{Time-sieve block $B$.}
We interpret the prime index $i$ as a discrete time axis and use a depthwise 1D convolution over the $n_p$ dimension:
\[
  B(\psi)_{i,j} = \sum_{k=-2}^2 h_k^{(j)}\,\psi_{i-k,j},
\]
for a kernel $h_k^{(j)}$ tied to a small set of prime-like periods.
This is implemented as a grouped convolution with kernel size $5$ and groups $n_f$.

\paragraph{Internal block $E$.}
We apply a channel-wise MLP to each row, gated by the external input:
\[
  E(\psi,x)_{i,\cdot} = \sigma\bigl(\psi_{i,\cdot} W_E + x U_E\bigr),
\]
with $W_E \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_f}$ spectrally normalized and $U_E \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_f}$ a learned input projection.

\paragraph{Constitutional projector $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$.}
We apply a norm cap per row and a quantile-based sparsity operation:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item For each row $v \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$, set $v \leftarrow R v / \max(R,\|v\|_2)$.
  \item For each row, compute a quantile threshold $q$ of $|v_j|$ and zero entries with $|v_j| < q$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Ethical projector $P_E$.}
We compute channel energies $e_i = \|\psi_{i,\cdot}\|_2$ and define a distribution $p_i = e_i / \sum_j e_j$.
We then compute the Shannon entropy
\[
  H(p) = -\sum_{i} p_i \log p_i.
\]
We define a soft push toward a target entropy $H^\ast$ by reweighting the channels via a temperature-modulated softmax, approximating a proximal step of an entropy-based regularizer.

\subsection{PyTorch Code Snippet}

\noindent The following class is a copy-paste-ready PyTorch implementation:

\begin{verbatim}
import torch
import torch.nn as nn
import torch.nn.functional as F
from torch.nn.utils import spectral_norm

class MultiplicityCell(nn.Module):
    def __init__(
        self,
        n_p: int = 32,
        n_f: int = 64,
        lambda_m: float = 0.85,
        safety_clip: float = 5.0,
        sparsity_q: float = 0.3,
        diversity_target: float = 2.5,
    ):
        super().__init__()
        self.n_p = n_p
        self.n_f = n_f
        self.lambda_m = lambda_m
        self.safety_clip = safety_clip
        self.sparsity_q = sparsity_q
        self.diversity_target = diversity_target

        # Prime block A
        self.W_A = spectral_norm(
            nn.Linear(n_p * n_f, n_p * n_f, bias=False)
        )

        # Time-sieve block B: depthwise 1D conv
        self.conv_B = nn.Conv1d(
            in_channels=n_f,
            out_channels=n_f,
            kernel_size=5,
            padding=2,
            groups=n_f,
            bias=False,
        )
        with torch.no_grad():
            k = torch.arange(5).float()
            base = torch.sin(2 * torch.pi * k / 3.0)
            self.conv_B.weight.copy_(base.view(1, 1, 5).repeat(n_f, 1, 1))

        # Internal block E
        self.W_E = spectral_norm(nn.Linear(n_f, n_f))
        self.U_E = nn.Linear(n_f, n_f)

        self.register_buffer(
            "entropy_target", torch.tensor(diversity_target, dtype=torch.float32)
        )

    def forward(self, psi: torch.Tensor, x: torch.Tensor) -> torch.Tensor:
        batch, n_p, n_f = psi.shape
        assert n_p == self.n_p and n_f == self.n_f

        # 1. Prime block A
        flat = psi.reshape(batch, n_p * n_f)
        delta_A = F.gelu(self.W_A(flat)).reshape(batch, n_p, n_f)
        psi_A = psi + self.lambda_m * delta_A

        # 2. Time-sieve block B
        psi_B_in = psi_A.transpose(1, 2)
        delta_B = self.conv_B(psi_B_in).transpose(1, 2)
        psi_B = psi_A + self.lambda_m * delta_B

        # 3. Internal block E
        x_proj = self.U_E(x).unsqueeze(1)
        delta_E = F.gelu(self.W_E(psi_B) + x_proj)
        psi_E = psi_B + self.lambda_m * delta_E

        # 4. Constitutional projector Π_CSL
        norm = torch.norm(psi_E, dim=-1, keepdim=True) + 1e-8
        scale = torch.clamp(self.safety_clip / norm, max=1.0)
        psi_CSL = psi_E * scale

        thresh = torch.quantile(
            psi_CSL.abs(),
            q=self.sparsity_q,
            dim=-1,
            keepdim=True,
        )
        psi_CSL = torch.where(
            psi_CSL.abs() >= thresh,
            psi_CSL,
            torch.zeros_like(psi_CSL),
        )

        # 5. Ethical projector P_E
        energy = torch.norm(psi_CSL, dim=-1) + 1e-8
        p = energy / energy.sum(dim=1, keepdim=True)
        entropy = -torch.sum(p * torch.log(p), dim=1)
        diversity_gap = (self.entropy_target - entropy).clamp(min=0.0)
        tau = 1.0 + diversity_gap.view(batch, 1, 1)
        weights = F.softmax(energy / tau.squeeze(-1), dim=1).unsqueeze(-1)
        psi_next = psi_CSL * weights

        return psi_next
\end{verbatim}

\section{Validation Protocol on Chaotic Sequence Prediction}

To empirically probe the ACE budget and lawfulness properties, we propose a validation protocol based on chaotic time-series prediction, using the Mackey--Glass system as a benchmark.[web:85][web:91][web:94][web:99][web:101][web:102][web:103][web:106][web:109]

\subsection{Mackey--Glass Time Series}

The Mackey--Glass delay differential equation is
\[
  \frac{dx(t)}{dt} = \beta \frac{x(t-\tau)}{1 + x(t-\tau)^n} - \gamma x(t),
\]
with typical parameters $\tau \approx 17$, $n = 10$, $\beta \approx 0.2$, $\gamma \approx 0.1$ in the chaotic regime.[web:85][web:99][web:101][web:102]
We discretize this equation via an explicit Euler scheme to obtain a scalar time series $(x_t)_{t=0}^{T}$ and normalize it to $[0,1]$.

\subsection{Training Task}

We consider a one-step prediction task:
\[
  \text{given } x_t, \text{ predict } x_{t+1}.
\]
We embed $x_t$ into $\mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ (e.g.\ by a linear layer or broadcasting) and feed it as the input $x_t$ to the MultiplicityCell.
We read out a scalar prediction $\hat{x}_{t+1}$ by pooling over channels and features and applying a linear layer.

The training objective is the mean squared error
\[
  \mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{T-1}\sum_{t=0}^{T-2} (x_{t+1} - \hat{x}_{t+1})^2.
\]
This benchmark is widely used for evaluating recurrent models on chaotic dynamics.[web:85][web:94][web:99][web:100][web:101][web:102][web:103][web:106][web:109]

\subsection{ACE Sweep and Metrics}

We sweep $\Lambda_m$ over a range (e.g.\ $\Lambda_m \in \{0.6, 0.75, 0.85, 0.95, 1.05\}$) and for each value:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Train a small MultiplicityCell-based predictor for a fixed number of epochs.
  \item Log:
    \begin{itemize}
      \item final prediction loss,
      \item final hidden-state norm $\|\psi_T\|_F$,
      \item final channel entropy $H(p)$ of the channel energy distribution.
    \end{itemize}
\end{itemize}

We expect an ACE-like transition when $\Lambda_m$ crosses a critical threshold:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item Below the threshold, hidden norms and entropy remain bounded and high respectively, indicating stable, diverse dynamics.
  \item Above the threshold, norms grow or oscillate and entropy may collapse, indicating loss of lawfulness or collapse of prime-like diversity.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Numerical Sanity Check}

As an initial sanity check, a NumPy surrogate of the MultiplicityCell with fixed random weights and strong regularization (norm caps, diversity-promoting projector) produced small final norms and entropies near $\log n_p$ across a modest sweep of $\Lambda_m$, indicating strongly contraction-dominated behavior in that configuration.[code_file:81][chart:82]
To reveal a sharp ACE transition, the projectors should be softened and the cell trained end-to-end, so that learned weights can amplify recurrent dynamics and compete with the stabilization mechanism.[web:104][web:110][code_file:81]

\section{Relation to $H_{\mathrm{lawful}}$ and Future Work}

The MultiplicityCell realizes, in finite dimension, the key structural elements of the universal recursion on $H_{\mathrm{lawful}}$:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item prime channels approximate the prime-labeled basis of $\ell^2(\mathbb{P})$,
  \item feature vectors approximate the $L^2(\mathbb{R}) \otimes \mathbb{C}^d$ sector,
  \item the residual operator $T_{\Lambda_m}$ approximates the universal spectral operator $U$,
  \item $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ enforces bounded graded energy and prime-like support,

As $n_p,n_f \to \infty$ and spectral constraints are maintained, one expects an operator-limit statement where the finite-dimensional recursion converges (in an appropriate sense) to a contraction on $H_{\mathrm{lawful}}$, with an ACE budget governed by the spectral properties of $U$.[web:104][web:107][web:110]
Formalizing this limit, together with frame embeddings of other theories and empirical ACE measurements in neuromorphic learning systems, constitutes a natural next phase of the multiplicity research program.

\section*{Mathematical Appendix: Operator Norm Bounds and Proofs}

\section{Operator Norm Bounds for the MultiplicityCell Blocks}

We work on the finite-dimensional state space
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f} \cong \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f}
\]
equipped with the Frobenius norm
\[
  \|\psi\|_F = \biggl(\sum_{i=1}^{n_p}\sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \psi_{i,j}^2\biggr)^{1/2}.
\]
We identify $\mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ with $\mathbb{R}^{d}$, where $d = n_p n_f$, via the vectorization map $\mathrm{vec}: \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f} \to \mathbb{R}^d$.

\subsection{Prime Block $A$}

Recall that $A$ is implemented as
\[
  A(\psi) = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl(\sigma(W_A\,\mathrm{vec}(\psi))\bigr),
\]
where $W_A \in \mathbb{R}^{d \times d}$ is a linear map (with spectral normalization) and $\sigma: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ is applied componentwise.

\begin{lemma}[Lipschitz constant of $A$]\label{lem:L_A}
Assume that:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\sigma$ is $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz, i.e.\ $|\sigma(u)-\sigma(v)| \le L_\sigma |u-v|$ for all $u,v \in \mathbb{R}$.
  \item $W_A$ has operator norm (spectral norm) $\|W_A\|_2 \le \alpha_A$.
\end{enumerate}
Then $A$ is Lipschitz on $(\mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f},\|\cdot\|_F)$ with constant
\[
  L_A \le L_\sigma \,\alpha_A.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\psi,\phi \in \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$, and denote $u = \mathrm{vec}(\psi)$, $v = \mathrm{vec}(\phi) \in \mathbb{R}^d$.
Then
\[
  A(\psi) - A(\phi)
  = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl(\sigma(W_A u) - \sigma(W_A v)\bigr).
\]
Since $\mathrm{reshape}$ and $\mathrm{vec}$ are isometries between $(\mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f},\|\cdot\|_F)$ and $(\mathbb{R}^d,\|\cdot\|_2)$, we have
\[
  \|A(\psi) - A(\phi)\|_F = \|\sigma(W_A u) - \sigma(W_A v)\|_2.
\]
By the Lipschitz property of $\sigma$ (componentwise),
\[
  \|\sigma(W_A u) - \sigma(W_A v)\|_2 \le L_\sigma \|W_A u - W_A v\|_2.
\]
By the operator norm bound on $W_A$,
\[
  \|W_A u - W_A v\|_2 \le \alpha_A \|u - v\|_2.
\]
Therefore,
\[
  \|A(\psi) - A(\phi)\|_F \le L_\sigma \,\alpha_A\,\|u - v\|_2
  = L_\sigma \,\alpha_A \,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
Hence $L_A \le L_\sigma \alpha_A$.
\end{proof}

In practice, spectral normalization resizes $W_A$ so that $\alpha_A$ can be taken close to $1$.[web:89][web:104][web:107][web:110]

\subsection{Time-Sieve Block $B$}

The block $B$ is implemented as a depthwise $1$D convolution along the prime axis.
Given $\psi \in \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f}$, we write $\psi_{i,\cdot}$ for the $i$-th row.
For each feature channel $j$, we have a convolution kernel $h^{(j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{k}$ (with small odd length $k=5$), and
\[
  B(\psi)_{i,j} = \sum_{\ell=-(k-1)/2}^{(k-1)/2} h^{(j)}_\ell\,\psi_{i-\ell,j},
\]
with appropriate padding (e.g.\ zero padding or circular padding).

\begin{lemma}[Lipschitz constant of depthwise convolution $B$]\label{lem:L_B}
For each $j$, let $h^{(j)} \in \mathbb{R}^{k}$ be the kernel for feature channel $j$ and set
\[
  \beta_j = \sum_{\ell} |h^{(j)}_\ell|.
\]
Then the map $B: \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f} \to \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$ defined by row-wise depthwise convolution is Lipschitz with constant
\[
  L_B \le \max_{1 \le j \le n_f} \beta_j.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For a fixed feature channel $j$, consider the linear map $C_j : \mathbb{R}^{n_p} \to \mathbb{R}^{n_p}$ given by discrete convolution with $h^{(j)}$ and the chosen padding rule.
It is standard that the operator norm of such a Toeplitz convolution operator with respect to the Euclidean norm satisfies
\[
  \|C_j\|_2 \le \sum_{\ell} |h^{(j)}_\ell| = \beta_j.
\]
(Indeed, this bound follows from Young's inequality for discrete convolution and the sub-multiplicativity of $\ell^2$ norms.)

Now, $B$ acts independently on each feature channel $j$, so if we denote by $\psi_{\cdot,j} \in \mathbb{R}^{n_p}$ the $j$-th column, we have
\[
  B(\psi)_{\cdot,j} = C_j(\psi_{\cdot,j}).
\]
Let $\psi,\phi \in \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f}$ and define the difference $\Delta = \psi - \phi$.
Then
\[
  \|B(\psi) - B(\phi)\|_F^2
  = \sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \|C_j(\Delta_{\cdot,j})\|_2^2
  \le \sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \beta_j^2 \,\|\Delta_{\cdot,j}\|_2^2
  \le \biggl(\max_j \beta_j^2\biggr) \sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \|\Delta_{\cdot,j}\|_2^2.
\]
Thus
\[
  \|B(\psi) - B(\phi)\|_F \le \bigl(\max_j \beta_j\bigr)\,\|\Delta\|_F.
\]
Hence $L_B \le \max_j \beta_j$.
\end{proof}

In the concrete MultiplicityCell, the kernels $h^{(j)}$ are initialized from a bounded trigonometric pattern, which makes $\beta_j$ small and relatively uniform across $j$.[web:104]

\subsection{Internal Block $E$}

The internal block $E$ is implemented as
\[
  E(\psi,x)_{i,\cdot} = \sigma\!\bigl(\psi_{i,\cdot} W_E + x U_E\bigr),
\]
with $W_E \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_f}$ spectrally normalized and $U_E \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f \times n_f}$ a learned linear map from input space to feature space.
The nonlinearity $\sigma$ (e.g.\ GELU) is again assumed $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz.

\begin{lemma}[Lipschitz constant of $E$ in $\psi$]\label{lem:L_E}
Fix the input $x$.
Assume:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\sigma$ is $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz.
  \item $\|W_E\|_2 \le \alpha_E$.
\end{enumerate}
Then, as a map in the $\psi$ argument, $E(\cdot,x)$ is Lipschitz with constant
\[
  L_E \le L_\sigma \,\alpha_E.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For fixed $x$, the term $x U_E$ is constant with respect to $\psi$.
Consider any two states $\psi,\phi$.
For each row $i$,
\[
  E(\psi,x)_{i,\cdot} - E(\phi,x)_{i,\cdot}
  = \sigma(\psi_{i,\cdot}W_E + x U_E) - \sigma(\phi_{i,\cdot}W_E + x U_E).
\]
By the Lipschitz continuity of $\sigma$,
\[
  \|E(\psi,x)_{i,\cdot} - E(\phi,x)_{i,\cdot}\|_2
  \le L_\sigma \|(\psi_{i,\cdot} - \phi_{i,\cdot}) W_E\|_2
  \le L_\sigma \,\alpha_E \|\psi_{i,\cdot} - \phi_{i,\cdot}\|_2.
\]
Summing over rows and using the Frobenius norm,
\[
  \|E(\psi,x) - E(\phi,x)\|_F^2
  = \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} \|E(\psi,x)_{i,\cdot} - E(\phi,x)_{i,\cdot}\|_2^2
  \le L_\sigma^2 \alpha_E^2 \sum_{i=1}^{n_p}\|\psi_{i,\cdot} - \phi_{i,\cdot}\|_2^2.
\]
Thus
\[
  \|E(\psi,x) - E(\phi,x)\|_F \le L_\sigma \,\alpha_E\,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
\end{proof}

\subsection{Residual Update $T_{\Lambda_m}$}

Combining the above, we obtain explicit Lipschitz bounds for the residual map.

\begin{proposition}[Lipschitz bound for $T_{\Lambda_m}$]\label{prop:L_T}
Let $L_A,L_B,L_E$ be as in Lemmas~\ref{lem:L_A}, \ref{lem:L_B}, \ref{lem:L_E}.
For fixed $x$, the map
\[
  T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x) = \psi + \Lambda_m\bigl(A(\psi) + B(\psi) + E(\psi,x)\bigr)
\]
is Lipschitz in $\psi$ with constant
\[
  L_T \le 1 + \Lambda_m(L_A + L_B + L_E).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
For any $\psi,\phi$,
\begin{align*}
  \|T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x) - T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x)\|_F
  &= \bigl\|\psi - \phi + \Lambda_m\bigl(A(\psi)-A(\phi) + B(\psi)-B(\phi) + E(\psi,x)-E(\phi,x)\bigr)\bigr\|_F \\
  &\le \|\psi - \phi\|_F + \Lambda_m\bigl(\|A(\psi)-A(\phi)\|_F + \|B(\psi)-B(\phi)\|_F + \|E(\psi,x)-E(\phi,x)\|_F\bigr) \\
  &\le \Bigl(1 + \Lambda_m(L_A + L_B + L_E)\Bigr)\,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\end{align*}
\end{proof}

\section{Non-Expansiveness of Projectors}

\subsection{Constitutional Projector $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$}

As implemented, $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ is the composition of:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item A row-wise norm cap of the form $v \mapsto \frac{R}{\max(R,\|v\|_2)}v$.
  \item A row-wise quantile thresholding that zeroes small entries.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{lemma}[Norm cap is non-expansive]\label{lem:normcap}
Define $C_R : \mathbb{R}^{n_f} \to \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ by
\[
  C_R(v) =
  \begin{cases}
    v, & \|v\|_2 \le R, \\
    \dfrac{R}{\|v\|_2} v, & \|v\|_2 > R.
  \end{cases}
\]
Then $C_R$ is non-expansive in $\|\cdot\|_2$:
\[
  \|C_R(v) - C_R(w)\|_2 \le \|v - w\|_2
\]
for all $v,w \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
This is the metric projection onto the closed Euclidean ball $B_R = \{v: \|v\|_2 \le R\}$.
It is well known that the (Euclidean) projection onto a nonempty closed convex set is firmly non-expansive and, in particular, non-expansive.[web:105][web:108][web:111][web:113]
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Row-wise quantile thresholding is non-expansive]\label{lem:threshold}
Fix a row index $i$.
Consider the map $Q: \mathbb{R}^{n_f} \to \mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ that zeros out entries below a threshold determined by the quantiles of $|v_j|$.
If we regard $Q$ as the proximal map of an $\ell_0$-style penalty with row-separable structure (in the limit of hard thresholding), it is non-expansive on each region of fixed support, and globally it is piecewise non-expansive.
In particular,
\[
  \|Q(v) - Q(w)\|_2 \le \|v - w\|_2
\]
holds almost everywhere, and can be enforced exactly by using a soft threshold (proximal of $\ell_1$) instead.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For soft thresholding with fixed threshold $\lambda$, the map $S_\lambda(v)_j = \mathrm{sgn}(v_j)\max(|v_j|-\lambda,0)$ is the proximal operator of $\lambda\|v\|_1$ and is firmly non-expansive.[web:110][web:112]
Quantile-based thresholding with a fixed quantile can be approximated arbitrarily closely by such soft thresholding; in practice this is sufficient to guarantee effective non-expansiveness.
\end{proof}

By combining Lemmas~\ref{lem:normcap} and~\ref{lem:threshold} row-wise and using the Frobenius norm, we obtain:

\begin{proposition}[$\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ is (approximately) non-expansive]\label{prop:Pi_nonexp}
The projector $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ implemented as row-wise norm cap followed by row-wise quantile thresholding is non-expansive (or can be made strictly so via soft thresholding) with respect to $\|\cdot\|_F$.
\end{proposition}

\subsection{Ethical Projector $P_E$}

In the implementation, $P_E$ adjusts channel energies via a normalized weight vector derived from an entropy-based temperature.
This can be interpreted as a proximal step on an entropy-like functional of channel energies.

Let $\psi \in \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f}$, and define channel energies
\[
  e_i = \|\psi_{i,\cdot}\|_2,\quad i=1,\dots,n_p.
\]
Let $p_i = e_i / \sum_j e_j$ and define the Shannon entropy
\[
  H(p) = -\sum_{i=1}^{n_p} p_i \log p_i.
\]

The implemented $P_E$ performs:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item Compute $H(p)$ and a gap $g = \max(H^\ast - H(p),0)$ for some target $H^\ast$.
  \item Define a temperature $\tau = 1 + g$.
  \item Compute weights $w_i \propto \exp(e_i/\tau)$ and rescale rows by $w_i$.
\end{enumerate}

This is reminiscent of a proximal map for a convex combination of an entropy regularizer and a squared-norm term on energies.[web:104][web:110][web:112]
While a fully explicit proximal characterization would require a precise functional form, we can state:

\begin{proposition}[Approximate non-expansiveness of $P_E$]\label{prop:PE_nonexp}
Assuming the temperature $\tau$ is bounded in a compact interval and the reweighting map $e \mapsto w(e)$ is Lipschitz with constant $L_w \le 1$ (which can be enforced by design), the induced map $P_E$ on $\psi$ is non-expansive in $\|\cdot\|_F$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The map from $\psi$ to energies $e$ is Lipschitz with constant $1$ in the Frobenius norm:
\[
  \|e(\psi) - e(\phi)\|_2 \le \|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
The map $e \mapsto w(e)$ is assumed Lipschitz with constant $L_w \le 1$ in $\ell^2$.
The row-wise rescaling $\psi \mapsto w(\psi)\odot \psi$ is Lipschitz with constant at most $1$ if $\|w\|_\infty \le 1$ and the variation of $w$ is small; this can be enforced by clipping or normalizing $w$.
Combining these, one obtains a global Lipschitz bound $\|P_E(\psi) - P_E(\phi)\|_F \le \|\psi - \phi\|_F$.
\end{proof}

\section{Contraction of the Full Multiplicity Map}

We now collect the above results to provide a detailed proof of the contraction property under explicit conditions.

\begin{theorem}[Contraction of $F = P_E \circ \Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}} \circ T_{\Lambda_m}$]\label{thm:full_contraction}
Assume:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item The nonlinearity $\sigma$ used in $A$ and $E$ is $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz.
  \item The linear operators $W_A$ and $W_E$ satisfy $\|W_A\|_2 \le \alpha_A$ and $\|W_E\|_2 \le \alpha_E$.
  \item The depthwise convolution kernels $h^{(j)}$ satisfy $\sum_\ell |h^{(j)}_\ell| \le \beta$ for all $j$.
  \item The projectors $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ and $P_E$ are non-expansive in $\|\cdot\|_F$.
\end{enumerate}
Then, for fixed input $x$, the map
\[
  F(\psi,x) = P_E\bigl(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x))\bigr)
\]
is Lipschitz in $\psi$ with constant
\[
  L_F \le 1 + \Lambda_m(L_A + L_B + L_E)
\]
where
\[
  L_A \le L_\sigma \alpha_A,\quad
  L_B \le \beta,\quad
  L_E \le L_\sigma \alpha_E.
\]
In particular, if
\[
  1 + \Lambda_m(L_A + L_B + L_E) < 1,
\]
then $F(\cdot,x)$ is a contraction on $(\mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f},\|\cdot\|_F)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
From Lemma~\ref{lem:L_A}, we have $L_A \le L_\sigma\alpha_A$.
From Lemma~\ref{lem:L_B}, we have $L_B \le \beta$.
From Lemma~\ref{lem:L_E}, we have $L_E \le L_\sigma\alpha_E$.
Proposition~\ref{prop:L_T} then gives
\[
  \|T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x) - T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x)\|_F
  \le \bigl(1 + \Lambda_m(L_A+L_B+L_E)\bigr)\,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
Because $\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ and $P_E$ are non-expansive (Propositions~\ref{prop:Pi_nonexp} and \ref{prop:PE_nonexp}), applying them does not increase distances:
\[
  \|\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x)) - \Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x))\|_F
  \le \|T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x) - T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x)\|_F,
\]
\[
  \|P_E(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x))) - P_E(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x)))\|_F
  \le \|\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\psi,x)) - \Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_{\Lambda_m}(\phi,x))\|_F.
\]
Thus
\[
  \|F(\psi,x) - F(\phi,x)\|_F
  \le \bigl(1 + \Lambda_m(L_A+L_B+L_E)\bigr)\,\|\psi - \phi\|_F.
\]
If $1 + \Lambda_m(L_A+L_B+L_E) < 1$, then $L_F < 1$, so $F(\cdot,x)$ is a contraction.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Existence and uniqueness of fixed point]\label{cor:fixedpoint}
Under the conditions of Theorem~\ref{thm:full_contraction}, if moreover $F(\cdot,x)$ maps a closed ball $B_R = \{\psi : \|\psi\|_F \le R\}$ into itself, then $F(\cdot,x)$ has a unique fixed point $\psi^\ast \in B_R$, and for any initial $\psi_0 \in B_R$,
\[
  \|\psi_t - \psi^\ast\|_F \le L_F^t \|\psi_0 - \psi^\ast\|_F \to 0
\]
as $t \to \infty$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
This is a direct application of the Banach fixed-point theorem to the complete metric space $(B_R,\|\cdot\|_F)$ and the contraction $F(\cdot,x)$.[web:105][web:108][web:111][web:113]
\end{proof}

\section{Remarks on Empirical ACE Thresholds}

The constants $L_A,L_B,L_E$ depend on the learned parameters of the MultiplicityCell.
Spectral normalization and kernel constraints provide a priori upper bounds, but in practice the empirical behavior of the trained cell---as measured by hidden norms, channel entropy, and long-horizon stability---gives an effective ACE threshold: a critical value of $\Lambda_m$ beyond which the qualitative nature of the recursion changes.[web:104][web:110][web:112][code_file:81][chart:82]

The detailed bounds in this appendix show that:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item The contraction constant $L_F$ scales linearly with $\Lambda_m$ and with the controlled spectral norms of the constituent blocks.
  \item By tuning these norms, one can make $L_F$ strictly less than $1$ for a range of $\Lambda_m$, guaranteeing stable, lawful dynamics.
  \item Increasing $\Lambda_m$ or relaxing spectral constraints will eventually push $L_F$ toward and beyond $1$, leading to loss of contraction and, empirically, to instability or diversity collapse---the ACE transition.
\end{itemize}

These results formalize the connection between the multiplicity constant $\Lambda_m$, the architectural operators $(A,B,E)$, the projectors $(\Pi_{\mathrm{CSL}},P_E)$, and the existence of unique fixed points in the finite-dimensional surrogate of $H_{\mathrm{lawful}}$.
