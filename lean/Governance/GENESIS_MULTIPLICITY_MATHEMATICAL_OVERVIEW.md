## 3. Typed Decomposition & PETC
**Prime-Exponent Tensor Calculus (PETC)** provides the bookkeeping layer for multiscalar interactions.

### 3.1 The Signature Monoid
Every typed operator is associated with a prime signature $e \in \mathbb{Z}^P$, where $P$ is the set of session primes. Composition of operators corresponds to addition in the signature monoid:
$$ e(U_1 \circ U_2) = e(U_1) + e(U_2) $$

### 3.2 Conservation Invariant
For any typed decomposition of an action $Y$ into sub-actions $X_i$, the system enforces exact additivity:
$$ e(Y) = \sum_{i} e(X_i) $$
This ensures that "multiplicity" (the count of prime eigenmodes) is conserved across the bridge, preventing semantic drift or "hallucinated" state.

$$ x^2 - Ny^2 = 1 $$

$$ m \equiv -a \cdot b^{-1} \pmod{|k|} $$
