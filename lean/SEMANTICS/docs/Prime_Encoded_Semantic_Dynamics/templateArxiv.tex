\section{Mathematical Overview}

\subsection{Prime-Indexed Fock Semantics}

The foundational idea is to represent semantic entities and their multiplicities as occupation patterns over prime-indexed modes.

\subsubsection{Prime modes and occupation numbers}

Let \(\mathbb{P}\) denote the set of prime numbers, and let each prime \(p \in \mathbb{P}\) correspond to a mode in a bosonic Fock-like space. An integer \(N > 1\) admits a unique prime factorization
\[
N = \prod_{p \in \mathbb{P}} p^{n_p}, \quad n_p \in \mathbb{N} \cup \{0\},
\]
which we interpret as the occupation vector
\[
\bm{n} = (n_2, n_3, n_5, \dots).
\]
This is a state \(|\bm{n}\rangle\) in a Fock basis indexed by primes.

The \emph{multiplicity operator} is then defined as
\begin{equation}
  M = \sum_{p \in \mathbb{P}} (\log p)\, n_p,
\end{equation}
which acts diagonally on number states, capturing the logarithmic weight of prime occupations. This operator is used as a central ``semantic mass'' or ``multiplicity potential'' in subsequent dynamics.

\subsubsection{Semantic states and density operators}

Semantic systems (words, capability tokens, traces) are modeled as states \(\rho\) on a finite-dimensional Hilbert space spanned by a truncated prime register. For computational tractability, an initial implementation uses the first \(K\) primes, yielding a Hilbert space of dimension \(d = 2^K\) for qubit encodings or higher if bosonic truncations are used.

A density operator \(\rho\) encodes the current semantic superposition or mixture over prime-indexed occupation patterns; semantic disambiguation corresponds to dynamical evolution of \(\rho\) under the master equation described below.

\subsection{Zeta-Driven Hamiltonian}

\subsubsection{Zeta zeros as resonant frequencies}

Let \(\zeta(s)\) be the Riemann zeta function, with nontrivial zeros \(\rho_k = \frac{1}{2} + i \gamma_k\) in the critical strip (assuming the usual conjectural behavior for conceptual motivation, though the construction does not depend on unproven properties). The imaginary parts \(\gamma_k\) are interpreted as resonant frequencies.

We define a Hamiltonian
\begin{equation}
  H_\zeta
  = \alpha \sum_{p} (\log p)\, n_p
    + \Re\left( \sum_{k} \frac{c_k}{\rho_k} e^{i \gamma_k M} \right),
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \(\alpha\) is a scaling parameter.
  \item \(M\) is the multiplicity operator defined above.
  \item Coefficients \(c_k\) and the truncation of the sum over \(k\) implement a Gaussian smoothing or cutoff of zeta zeros.
\end{itemize}

The first term encodes a static multiplicity potential; the second term introduces oscillatory driving at frequencies tied to the zeta zeros, effectively inducing temporal interference patterns in the prime register.

\subsubsection{Lindblad dissipators}

Open-system behavior, decoherence, and semantic collapse are captured by Lindblad dissipators
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho
  = L_k \rho L_k^\dagger
    - \frac{1}{2} \{ L_k^\dagger L_k, \rho \},
\end{equation}
for suitable operators \(L_k\) that may be prime-indexed, context-conditioned, or norm-preserving in chosen bases. These terms allow us to model relaxation towards more definite semantic states (e.g., disambiguation) under the combined influence of \(H_\zeta\) and environmental interactions.

\section{Preliminaries and Notation}

We work on a finite-dimensional Hilbert space \(\mathcal{H}\) constructed from a truncated prime register. Let
\[
\mathbb{P}_K = \{p_1, p_2, \dots, p_K\}
\]
denote the first \(K\) prime numbers. We typically take \(\mathcal{H}\) to be either:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item a qubit register of dimension \(d = 2^K\), encoding each prime mode as a two-level system; or
  \item a truncated bosonic Fock space with local cutoff \(n_{\max} \in \mathbb{N}\), giving \(d = (n_{\max}+1)^K\).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Number Operators and Multiplicity Operator}

For each prime \(p_j \in \mathbb{P}_K\), we define a number operator \(n_{p_j}\) on \(\mathcal{H}\) satisfying
\[
n_{p_j} |n_1, \dots, n_j, \dots, n_K\rangle = n_j\, |n_1, \dots, n_j, \dots, n_K\rangle,
\]
with \(n_j \in \{0, 1\}\) in a qubit model or \(n_j \in \{0, 1, \dots, n_{\max}\}\) in a truncated bosonic model.

The \emph{multiplicity operator} is
\begin{equation}
  M = \sum_{j=1}^K (\log p_j)\, n_{p_j}.  \label{eq:M-def}
\end{equation}
Since each \(n_{p_j}\) is positive semidefinite and diagonal in the occupation basis, \(M\) is self-adjoint and positive semidefinite.

\subsection{Norms and Basic Bounds}

We use the operator norm \(\|\cdot\|\) induced by the Hilbert space norm on \(\mathcal{H}\). For any self-adjoint operator \(A\) with spectrum contained in \([a, b]\), we have
\[
\|A\| = \max\{|a|, |b|\} = \max_{\psi \neq 0} \frac{\langle\psi, A\psi\rangle}{\|\psi\|^2}.
\]

For \(M\) as in \eqref{eq:M-def}, if we assume a uniform local cut-off \(n_j \le n_{\max}\), the maximum eigenvalue of \(M\) is
\begin{equation}
  \lambda_{\max}(M)
  = \sum_{j=1}^K (\log p_j) n_{\max}
  = n_{\max} \sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j,                                  \label{eq:M-lambda-max}
\end{equation}
and \(\lambda_{\min}(M) = 0\). Hence
\begin{equation}
  \|M\| = \lambda_{\max}(M) = n_{\max} \sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j.        \label{eq:M-norm-bound}
\end{equation}
In the qubit case \((n_{\max} = 1)\), this simplifies to
\[
\|M\| \le \sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j.
\]

\section{Bounds on the Zeta-Driven Hamiltonian}

Recall the zeta-driven Hamiltonian
\begin{equation}
  H_\zeta
  = \alpha \sum_{j=1}^K (\log p_j)\, n_{p_j}
    + \Re\!\left( \sum_{k=1}^N \frac{c_k}{\rho_k} e^{i \gamma_k M} \right), \label{eq:Hzeta}
\end{equation}
where \(\rho_k = \frac{1}{2} + i\gamma_k\) are truncated nontrivial zeta zeros, \(\gamma_k \in \mathbb{R}\), and \(N\) is a finite cutoff. We assume coefficients \(c_k \in \mathbb{C}\) and real \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\).

\subsection{Bound on the Multiplicity Term}

The first term in \eqref{eq:Hzeta} is
\[
H_M := \alpha M = \alpha \sum_{j=1}^K (\log p_j)\, n_{p_j}.
\]
By \eqref{eq:M-norm-bound},
\begin{equation}
  \|H_M\| = |\alpha|\,\|M\|
           \le |\alpha|\, n_{\max} \sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j.   \label{eq:Hm-bound}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Bound on the Zeta-Oscillatory Term}

Define
\[
S := \sum_{k=1}^N \frac{c_k}{\rho_k} e^{i \gamma_k M}.
\]
Since \(M\) is self-adjoint, the spectral theorem yields
\[
e^{i \gamma_k M}
= \sum_{\lambda \in \sigma(M)} e^{i \gamma_k \lambda} P_\lambda,
\]
with spectral projections \(P_\lambda\). It follows that \(e^{i \gamma_k M}\) is unitary, hence
\[
\|e^{i \gamma_k M}\| = 1.
\]
Therefore
\begin{equation}
  \|S\|
  \le \sum_{k=1}^N \left\|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k} e^{i \gamma_k M}\right\|
  =  \sum_{k=1}^N \left|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k}\right|.  \label{eq:S-bound}
\end{equation}

The operator of interest in \eqref{eq:Hzeta} is the real part
\[
H_{\mathrm{osc}} := \Re(S) = \frac{1}{2}(S + S^\dagger).
\]
Using \(\|S^\dagger\| = \|S\|\) and the triangle inequality,
\[
\|H_{\mathrm{osc}}\|
  = \left\|\frac{S + S^\dagger}{2}\right\|
  \le \frac{1}{2}\bigl(\|S\| + \|S^\dagger\|\bigr)
  = \|S\|.
\]
Hence, from \eqref{eq:S-bound},
\begin{equation}
  \|H_{\mathrm{osc}}\|
  \le \sum_{k=1}^N \left|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k}\right|. \label{eq:Hosc-bound}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Combined Bound for \texorpdfstring{$H_\zeta$}{Hζ}}

Combining \eqref{eq:Hm-bound} and \eqref{eq:Hosc-bound}, and using the triangle inequality for operator norms,
\begin{equation}
  \|H_\zeta\|
  \le \|H_M\| + \|H_{\mathrm{osc}}\|
  \le |\alpha|\,n_{\max}\sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j
     + \sum_{k=1}^N \left|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k}\right|. \label{eq:Hzeta-norm-bound}
\end{equation}
This bound is explicit in terms of:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item the truncation level \(K\) on primes,
  \item the local occupation cutoff \(n_{\max}\),
  \item the coefficient \(\alpha\),
  \item and the truncated set of zeta zeros and weights \(\{(c_k,\rho_k)\}_{k=1}^N\).
\end{itemize}

\section{Bounds for the Full Generator}

The unified master equation is
\begin{equation}
  \dot{\rho}
  = \mathcal{L}(\rho)
  := -i\bigl[H_\zeta + \beta\,\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t), \rho\bigr]
     + \sum_k \mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho
     + \Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\bigl(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho) - \rho\bigr).
  \label{eq:L-def}
\end{equation}
We analyze the induced operator norm of \(\mathcal{L}\) on the space of trace-class operators endowed with the trace norm \(\|\cdot\|_1\) or Hilbert--Schmidt norm \(\|\cdot\|_2\); the latter is often more convenient for explicit bounds in finite dimensions.

\subsection{Hamiltonian Commutator Term}

Consider the Hamiltonian part
\[
\mathcal{L}_H(\rho) := -i[H, \rho], \quad H := H_\zeta + \beta \Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t).
\]

\subsubsection{Bound in Hilbert--Schmidt Norm}

Let \(\|\cdot\|_2\) denote the Hilbert--Schmidt norm,
\[
\|X\|_2 := \sqrt{\operatorname{Tr}(X^\dagger X)}.
\]
For bounded operators \(A,B\), the inequality
\[
\|[A,B]\|_2 \le 2\|A\|\,\|B\|_2
\]
holds. Indeed,
\[
\|AB\|_2 \le \|A\|\|B\|_2,\quad \|BA\|_2 \le \|A\|\|B\|_2,
\]
so
\[
\|[A,B]\|_2 = \|AB-BA\|_2
  \le \|AB\|_2 + \|BA\|_2
  \le 2\|A\|\|B\|_2.
\]
Thus,
\begin{equation}
  \|\mathcal{L}_H(\rho)\|_2
  = \|[H,\rho]\|_2
  \le 2 \|H\|\,\|\rho\|_2. \label{eq:L-HS-bound}
\end{equation}

Here
\[
\|H\| \le \|H_\zeta\| + |\beta|\,\|\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t)\|,
\]
with \(\|H_\zeta\|\) bounded as in \eqref{eq:Hzeta-norm-bound}. If we assume a uniform bound \(\|\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t)\| \le C_{\Xi}\) for all \(t\), then
\begin{equation}
  \|H\|
  \le |\alpha|\,n_{\max}\sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j
     + \sum_{k=1}^N \left|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k}\right|
     + |\beta|\,C_{\Xi}. \label{eq:H-total-bound}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Lindblad Dissipators}

Each Lindblad term has the form
\[
\mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho
= L_k\rho L_k^\dagger - \frac{1}{2}\{L_k^\dagger L_k, \rho\},
\]
with \(\{A,B\} = AB + BA\). In Hilbert--Schmidt norm, we can bound
\begin{align*}
  \|\mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho\|_2
  &\le \|L_k \rho L_k^\dagger\|_2
       + \frac{1}{2}\|L_k^\dagger L_k \rho\|_2
       + \frac{1}{2}\|\rho L_k^\dagger L_k\|_2 \\
  &\le \|L_k\|^2 \|\rho\|_2
       + \frac{1}{2}\|L_k\|^2 \|\rho\|_2
       + \frac{1}{2}\|L_k\|^2 \|\rho\|_2 \\
  &= 2\|L_k\|^2\,\|\rho\|_2.
\end{align*}
Summing over \(k\),
\begin{equation}
  \left\|\sum_k \mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho\right\|_2
  \le 2\left(\sum_k \|L_k\|^2\right) \|\rho\|_2. \label{eq:Lindblad-bound}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Multiplicity Projection Term}

The stabilization term is
\[
\mathcal{L}_{\Lambda}(\rho)
:= \Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\bigl(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho) - \rho\bigr).
\]
Let us assume:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \(\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\) is uniformly bounded: \(\|\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\| \le C_{\Lambda}\) for all \(t\).
  \item \(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}\) is a contraction in the Hilbert--Schmidt norm:
        \(\|\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho)\|_2 \le \|\rho\|_2\).
\end{itemize}
Then
\begin{align*}
  \|\mathcal{L}_{\Lambda}(\rho)\|_2
  &= \|\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\bigl(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho) - \rho\bigr)\|_2 \\
  &\le \|\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\|\,
       \|\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho) - \rho\|_2 \\
  &\le C_{\Lambda} \bigl( \|\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho)\|_2 + \|\rho\|_2 \bigr) \\
  &\le 2 C_{\Lambda} \|\rho\|_2.
\end{align*}
Thus,
\begin{equation}
  \|\mathcal{L}_{\Lambda}(\rho)\|_2
  \le 2 C_{\Lambda} \|\rho\|_2. \label{eq:Lambda-bound}
\end{equation}

\subsection{Combined Generator Bound}

Combining \eqref{eq:L-HS-bound}, \eqref{eq:Lindblad-bound}, and \eqref{eq:Lambda-bound}, we find that the full generator \(\mathcal{L}\) satisfies, in Hilbert--Schmidt norm,
\begin{equation}
  \|\mathcal{L}(\rho)\|_2
  \le \left( 2\|H\|
           + 2\sum_k \|L_k\|^2
           + 2C_{\Lambda}
      \right)\|\rho\|_2.   \label{eq:L-total-bound}
\end{equation}
Substituting \eqref{eq:H-total-bound},
\begin{align}
  \|\mathcal{L}(\rho)\|_2
  &\le
    2\Bigl(
       |\alpha|\,n_{\max}\sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j
       + \sum_{k=1}^N \left|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k}\right|
       + |\beta|\,C_{\Xi}
    \Bigr)\|\rho\|_2 \nonumber \\
  &\quad + 2\left(\sum_k \|L_k\|^2 + C_{\Lambda}\right)\|\rho\|_2. \label{eq:L-total-bound-expanded}
\end{align}
Equation \eqref{eq:L-total-bound-expanded} gives an explicit, finite-dimensional operator norm bound for the PESD generator in terms of:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item prime truncation \(K\) and cut-off \(n_{\max}\),
  \item coefficients \(\alpha, \beta\),
  \item semantic drive bound \(C_{\Xi}\),
  \item zeta coefficients \(c_k\) and truncated zeros \(\rho_k\),
  \item dissipator norms \(\|L_k\|\),
  \item and multiplicity stabilizer bound \(C_{\Lambda}\).
\end{itemize}

\section{Contractivity and Small-Gain Condition}

We now relate the small-gain condition \(\beta < 1\) and the certification hook \(q_t + \eta_t < 1 - \varepsilon\) to a contractivity property of the stabilized dynamics.

\subsection{Perturbation Decomposition}

Write the generator as
\[
\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_0 + \mathcal{L}_{\beta},
\]
where
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \(\mathcal{L}_0\) collects the terms with \(\beta = 0\):
    \[
      \mathcal{L}_0(\rho)
      := -i[H_\zeta,\rho]
         + \sum_k \mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho
         + \Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho) - \rho),
    \]
  \item \(\mathcal{L}_{\beta}\) collects the perturbation from the semantic drive:
    \[
      \mathcal{L}_{\beta}(\rho)
      := -i\beta[\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t), \rho].
    \]
\end{itemize}

In Hilbert--Schmidt norm, from \eqref{eq:L-HS-bound},
\[
\|\mathcal{L}_{\beta}(\rho)\|_2
\le 2|\beta|\,\|\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t)\|\,\|\rho\|_2
\le 2|\beta| C_{\Xi}\,\|\rho\|_2.
\]

\subsection{Nominal Contractivity}

Suppose we can show that the nominal generator \(\mathcal{L}_0\) is \emph{contractive} in Hilbert--Schmidt norm, i.e., there exists \(\lambda > 0\) such that
\begin{equation}
  \|\mathcal{L}_0(\rho)\|_2
  \le -\lambda \|\rho - \rho^\star\|_2 \label{eq:L0-contractivity}
\end{equation}
for some fixed point \(\rho^\star\) (e.g., a stationary multiplicity-stabilized state). This is a structural assumption about the choice of \(H_\zeta\), \(\{L_k\}\), and \(\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}, \Pi_{\Lambda_m}\)---for instance, \(\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}\) may induce decay towards a low-dimensional manifold.

Under this assumption, the full dynamics satisfies
\[
\frac{d}{dt}\|\rho(t) - \rho^\star\|_2
\le -\lambda \|\rho(t) - \rho^\star\|_2
    + \|\mathcal{L}_\beta(\rho(t))\|_2.
\]

Using the bound on \(\mathcal{L}_\beta\), we obtain
\begin{equation}
  \frac{d}{dt}\|\rho(t) - \rho^\star\|_2
  \le -\lambda \|\rho(t) - \rho^\star\|_2
      + 2|\beta| C_{\Xi}\,\|\rho(t)\|_2. \label{eq:small-gain-ineq}
\end{equation}
Assuming \(\|\rho(t)\|_2\) is bounded (which holds in finite dimensions for density matrices), we can treat the perturbation as a small-gain input.

\subsection{Small-Gain Bound and Certification Hook}

In a small-gain framework, one typically defines:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item an ``internal'' gain \(q\) capturing the effect of \(\mathcal{L}_0\) on deviations \(\rho - \rho^\star\),
  \item an ``input'' gain \(\eta\) capturing the amplification of the perturbation term \(\mathcal{L}_\beta\).
\end{itemize}

At the discrete-time level (e.g., for a step-size \(h > 0\) and an Euler-like update),
\[
\rho_{t+1} \approx \rho_t + h\,\mathcal{L}(\rho_t),
\]
one may obtain a bound of the form
\begin{equation}
  \|\rho_{t+1} - \rho^\star\|_2
  \le q_t \|\rho_t - \rho^\star\|_2 + \eta_t\,\|\rho_t\|_2,
\end{equation}
for appropriately defined \(q_t,\eta_t\) depending on \(\lambda, \beta, h, C_{\Xi}\). The \emph{certification hook}
\[
q_t + \eta_t < 1 - \varepsilon
\]
then ensures that, recursively,
\[
\|\rho_{t+1} - \rho^\star\|_2
\le (1 - \varepsilon)\,C\,,
\]
for some bound \(C\), preventing unbounded growth.

In our architecture:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item The choice \(\beta = 0.8 < 1\) is designed so that the perturbation gain is strictly below the nominal contraction margin.
  \item The condition \(q_t + \eta_t < 1 - \varepsilon\), with \(\varepsilon = 0.01\), is enforced as a hard circuit-breaker in the numerical test, causing CI failure if violated.
\end{itemize}

A fully explicit derivation of \(q_t\) and \(\eta_t\) requires a particular discretization scheme and concrete bounds on \(\|\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}(t)\|\) and the dissipative/stabilizing structure of \(\mathcal{L}_0\); the architecture records the qualitative relation (nominal contraction plus bounded perturbation) and implements the inequality as a numerically checked constraint.

\section{Stability Under \texorpdfstring{$\Lambda_m$}{Λm}-Projection}

We briefly discuss the effect of the \(\Lambda_m\)-projection term on stability.

\subsection{Projection as Non-Expansive Map}

Assume \(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}\colon \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H}) \to \mathcal{B}(\mathcal{H})\) satisfies
\[
\|\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho_1) - \Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho_2)\|_2
\le \|\rho_1 - \rho_2\|_2,
\]
for all \(\rho_1,\rho_2\). That is, \(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}\) is non-expansive in Hilbert--Schmidt norm. This is plausible if \(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}\) is an orthogonal projection in the Hilbert--Schmidt inner product or a conditional expectation onto a subalgebra.

\subsection{Dissipative Effect of the Stabilizer}

The term
\[
\mathcal{L}_{\Lambda}(\rho)
= \Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\bigl(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho) - \rho\bigr)
\]
can be seen as enforcing relaxation towards the set
\[
\mathcal{M} := \{\rho \colon \rho = \Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho)\},
\]
which we interpret as the multiplicity-stabilized manifold.

To see the dissipative flavor, consider the quadratic functional
\[
V(\rho) := \|\rho - \Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho)\|_2^2.
\]
Differentiating along trajectories of \(\dot{\rho} = \mathcal{L}_{\Lambda}(\rho)\) (and ignoring the Hamiltonian/Lindblad parts for this local analysis) gives, formally,
\[
\frac{d}{dt} V(\rho(t))
= 2\,\Re\bigl\langle \rho(t) - \Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho(t)),
   \mathcal{L}_{\Lambda}(\rho(t)) \bigr\rangle_2,
\]
where \(\langle X, Y\rangle_2 := \operatorname{Tr}(X^\dagger Y)\). If \(\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}\) is negative semidefinite on the difference \(\rho - \Pi_{\Lambda_m}(\rho)\), then
\[
\frac{d}{dt} V(\rho(t)) \le 0,
\]
ensuring that trajectories do not move away from \(\mathcal{M}\). In practice, one would choose \(\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}\) to be of the form
\[
\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t) = -\kappa(t)\,I,
\]
with \(\kappa(t) > 0\) suitably bounded, or another operator inducing decay in directions orthogonal to \(\mathcal{M}\).

\section{Summary of Operator Norm Structure}

Collecting the key results:

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*), nosep]
  \item The multiplicity operator \(M\) has explicit norm bound
    \[
      \|M\| \le n_{\max}\sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j.
    \]
  \item The zeta-driven Hamiltonian satisfies
    \[
      \|H_\zeta\|
      \le |\alpha|\,n_{\max}\sum_{j=1}^K \log p_j
         + \sum_{k=1}^N \left|\frac{c_k}{\rho_k}\right|.
    \]
  \item The Hamiltonian commutator term is bounded in Hilbert--Schmidt norm by
    \[
      \|\mathcal{L}_H(\rho)\|_2
      \le 2\|H\|\,\|\rho\|_2,
    \]
    with \(\|H\|\) bounded as above plus the semantic-drive contribution.
  \item Lindblad dissipators satisfy
    \[
      \left\|\sum_k \mathcal{D}[L_k]\rho\right\|_2
      \le 2\left(\sum_k \|L_k\|^2\right)\|\rho\|_2.
    \]
  \item The multiplicity projection term satisfies
    \[
      \|\mathcal{L}_\Lambda(\rho)\|_2
      \le 2 C_{\Lambda} \|\rho\|_2,
    \]
    under uniform boundedness of \(\Lambda_m^{\mathrm{op}}(t)\) and non-expansivity of \(\Pi_{\Lambda_m}\).
  \item The full generator admits
    \[
      \|\mathcal{L}(\rho)\|_2
      \le K_{\mathrm{tot}}\,\|\rho\|_2
    \]
    with explicitly computable \(K_{\mathrm{tot}}\) in terms of \(\alpha,\beta, K, n_{\max}, c_k,\rho_k, L_k, C_{\Xi},C_\Lambda\).
  \item Under additional structural assumptions on \(\mathcal{L}_0\) and \(\Xi_{\mathrm{sem}}\), the small-gain condition \(q_t + \eta_t < 1 - \varepsilon\) with \(\beta = 0.8\) enforces contractivity of the discretized dynamics and justifies its use as a circuit-breaker in numerical certification.
\end{enumerate}

These bounds and structural conditions formalize the intuition that the PESD/ZPSOS dynamics, when properly parameterized and stabilized by \(\Lambda_m\), define a lawful and controllable evolution in a prime-indexed semantic space.
