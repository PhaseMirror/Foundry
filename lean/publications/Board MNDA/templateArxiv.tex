\textbf{Parties.} This Board / Trustee Non-Disclosure Agreement (the ``\textbf{Agreement}'') is entered into as of (the ``\textbf{Effective Date}'') by and between \textbf{Citizen Gardens}, an international nonprofit organization with a corporate office at \address{189 Private Dr. 123, Crown City, Ohio 45632} CG, and _____________________________________________, with an address at _____________________________________________ (the Recipient).

\textbf{1. Purpose.} Recipient will receive access to non-public information to fulfill board/trustee duties for CG. This Agreement governs the use, protection, and return of that information.

\textbf{2. Confidential Information.} ``\textbf{Confidential Information}'' means all non-public information disclosed by or on behalf of CG in any form, including board materials, agendas, minutes, strategies, financials, roadmaps, legal and personnel information, security information, incident reports, and third-party information CG is obligated to protect under a corporate mutual NDA (``\textbf{Corporate MNDA}''), and any notes, analyses, or derivatives.

\textbf{3. Exclusions.} Confidential Information does not include information that Recipient can demonstrate: (a) is or becomes public through no breach; (b) was known to Recipient without restriction before disclosure; (c) is independently developed without use of Confidential Information; or (d) is rightfully received from a third party without duty of confidentiality.

\textbf{4. Use and Non-Disclosure.} Recipient will use Confidential Information only for CG board/trustee duties and will not disclose it to any person except CG-authorized personnel or advisors who have a need to know and are bound by obligations no less protective. Recipient will implement reasonable administrative, physical, and technical safeguards, including device hygiene (work accounts only, disk encryption, screen lock, anti-phishing measures) and least-privilege access.

\textbf{5. No-Residuals.} Memory use counts as use. Recipient will not use CG Confidential Information, including any retained in unaided memory, for any purpose other than CG duties. Independent development is permitted only with contemporaneous, objective evidence created without use of Confidential Information.

\textbf{6. Regulated Data Prohibition.} Confidential Information will \emph{not} include regulated data such as PHI, student records, children’s data, or other PII that requires special terms. If such data is contemplated, CG and Recipient must have appropriate addenda in place (e.g., BAA/DUA/FERPA) before access.

\textbf{7. Third-Party Information.} When Recipient accesses a partner’s information under a Corporate MNDA, the Corporate MNDA governs that information; this Agreement governs CG’s information. If terms conflict, the stricter obligation controls.

\textbf{8. Conflicts of Interest.} Recipient will comply with CG’s conflict-of-interest and safeguarding policies, timely disclose potential conflicts, and recuse where appropriate.

\textbf{9. Compelled Disclosure.} If Recipient is legally required to disclose Confidential Information, Recipient will provide prompt written notice to CG (to the extent lawful) and cooperate to seek protective treatment. Only the legally required portion may be disclosed.

\textbf{10. Return and Deletion.} Upon CG’s request or upon the end of service, Recipient will promptly cease use, return or securely delete all Confidential Information and certify completion in writing. Routine archival backups may be destroyed in the ordinary course.

\textbf{11. Term.} This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues during Recipient’s service and for three (3) years thereafter. Obligations for trade secrets and regulated data continue as long as such information remains protected under applicable law.

\textbf{12. No Publicity; No Assignment.} Recipient will not use CG’s name or logos without prior written consent. Recipient may not assign this Agreement without CG’s prior written consent.

\textbf{13. Remedies.} Unauthorized use or disclosure may cause irreparable harm. CG is entitled to equitable relief (including injunction) in addition to other remedies at law or in equity.

\textbf{14. Miscellaneous.} This Agreement is the entire agreement between the parties regarding its subject and supersedes inconsistent prior understandings on confidentiality for Recipient’s board/trustee role. Amendments must be in writing signed by both parties. Electronic signatures and counterparts are valid. Notices to CG: Citizen Gardens, Attn: Board Secretary, 189 Private Dr. 123, Crown City, Ohio 45632.
