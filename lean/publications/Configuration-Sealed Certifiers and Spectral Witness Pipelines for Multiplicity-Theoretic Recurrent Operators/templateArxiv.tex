\begin{abstract}
We introduce the notion of a \emph{Configuration-Sealed Certifier} (CSC) for
Multiplicity-Theoretic dynamical systems, and formalize an end-to-end
spectral certification pipeline for a zeta-specialized recurrent operator
(ZetaCell) coupled to a Pro-tier stability certifier and an oracle veto
policy. The pipeline combines finite-dimensional MultiplicityCell and
ZetaCell operators, a SpectralWitness object encoding zeta-zero spacing
statistics, a Pro-tier contraction certificate, and a two-key UnifiedProWitness
that binds mathematical and governance evidence. We further define a Tier~4
density layer for Phase Mirror Pro based on GUE nearest-neighbor spacing
tests, and sketch a language-agnostic library structure for implementing
CSC-based certifiers in strongly typed languages. This article is intended as
prior art and defensive publication.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Multiplicity Theory models lawful dynamics on an ambient multiplicity
Hilbert space with prime-labeled structure and universal spectral
operators acting on prime and auxiliary sectors.
Finite-dimensional surrogates, such as the MultiplicityCell and ZetaCell,
realize these dynamics as spectrally constrained recurrent operators
equipped with constitutional and ethical projectors.
The present work extends this formalism in three directions:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
  \item A formal definition of \emph{Configuration-Sealed Certifiers} (CSC)
        which are parameterized but non-interchangeable certifiers whose
        configuration is sealed at construction time;
  \item A Pro-tier spectral pipeline for ZetaCell, including a
        SpectralWitness object, a Pro-tier FormalStabilityCertificate,
        and a UnifiedProWitness implementing a two-key veto protocol;
  \item A Tier~4 density mechanism for Phase Mirror Pro based on GUE
        nearest-neighbor spacing tests on zeta-zero distributions.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Recap: MultiplicityCell and ZetaCell}

We briefly recall the MultiplicityCell and ZetaCell architectures as
finite-dimensional surrogates of a universal multiplicity recursion.%
\footnote{See the attached MultiplicityCell manuscript for full details.}[file:2]

\subsection{MultiplicityCell}

Let $n_p, n_f \in \mathbb{N}$ and define the finite-dimensional state space
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}
  = \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f},
  \quad
  \|\Psi\|_F^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} \sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \Psi_{i,j}^2.
\]
We interpret the $i$-th row as a feature vector attached to the $i$-th
prime channel.%
[file:2]

The MultiplicityCell realizes an update of the form
\[
  \Psi_{t+1} = P_E P_{\mathrm{CSL}} T_m(\Psi_t, x_t),
  \quad
  T_m(\Psi, x) = \Psi + m \, U(\Psi, x),
\]
where $U$ decomposes into a prime block $A$, a time-sieve block $B$, and
an internal block $E$, each with Lipschitz constants $L_A, L_B, L_E$.
The constitutional projector $P_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ enforces a lawfulness
budget via norm caps and sparsity, while the ethical projector $P_E$
promotes channel-diversity through entropy-based reweighting.%
[file:2]

Under spectral-norm constraints and a multiplicity constant $m$ satisfying
\[
  1 + m \bigl(L_A + L_B + L_E\bigr) < 1,
\]
the composite map acts as a contraction on a suitable ball in
$\mathcal{H}_{n_p,n_f}$, yielding a unique fixed point by the Banach
fixed-point theorem and an empirical Absolute Contraction Energy (ACE)
budget.%
[file:2]

\subsection{ZetaCell}

ZetaCell extends the state space to a dual prime--zero sector%
[file:1]
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{N,M}
  = \mathcal{H}_p^{(N)} \times \mathcal{H}_z^{(M)},\quad
  \mathcal{H}_p^{(N)} = \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f},\quad
  \mathcal{H}_z^{(M)} = \mathbb{R}^{n_z \times n_g},
\]
with rows in $\mathcal{H}_p^{(N)}$ indexed by truncated primes
$p_1,\dots,p_{n_p}$ and rows in $\mathcal{H}_z^{(M)}$ indexed by the
first $n_z$ nontrivial zeta zeros $\tfrac12 + i\gamma_k$.%
[file:1]

The ZetaCell recursion has the same constitutional form
\[
  (\Psi_{t+1}, \Chi_{t+1})
  = P_E P_{\mathrm{CSL}} T_m\bigl((\Psi_t,\Chi_t), x_t\bigr),
\]
but with $U$ decomposed as
\begin{align*}
  U_p(\Psi,\Chi,x) &= A_p(\Psi) + C_{zp}(\Chi) + B_p(\Psi,\Chi) + E_{p}(\Psi,\Chi, x),\\
  U_z(\Psi,\Chi,x) &= A_z(\Chi) + C_{pz}(\Psi) + B_z(\Psi,\Chi) + E_{z}(\Psi,\Chi, x),
\end{align*}
where $C_{pz}, C_{zp}$ are bridge operators built from an oscillatory
kernel inspired by explicit formulas for prime-counting functions:%
[file:1]
\[
  K_{ik} = a_{ik} \cos(\gamma_k \log p_i) + b_{ik} \sin(\gamma_k \log p_i),
  \quad 1 \le i \le n_p,\; 1 \le k \le n_z.
\]
The bridge acts via learnable maps $R_{pz}$ and $R_{zp}$.

Lipschitz bounds for each block yield an ACE-style contraction criterion
similar to the prime-only case, with additional contributions from the
bridge operators.%
[file:1]

\section{SpectralWitness and Zero-Spacing Metrics}

To certify the spectral health of the prime--zero bridge, we define a
SpectralWitness object associated to a fixed $n_0$-zero bridge kernel.

Let $\gamma_1 < \gamma_2 < \cdots < \gamma_{n_0}$ denote the truncated
zeta zero heights and $\{p_i\}_{i=1}^{n_p}$ the truncated primes used in
the bridge. Consider the bridge kernel
\[
  K_{ik} = \cos(\gamma_k \log p_i),
  \quad 1 \le i \le n_p,\; 1 \le k \le n_0.
\]

\begin{definition}[SpectralWitness]
A SpectralWitness for a fixed bridge kernel $K$ is a tuple
\[
  W = (\delta_{pz}, \|R_{pz}\|_2, r_{\mathrm{bridge}}, n_0,
       \{s_k\}_{k=1}^{n_0-1}, \tau_{\mathrm{gap}}),
\]
where:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item $\delta_{pz} = \min_{1 \le k < n_0} (\gamma_{k+1}-\gamma_k)$ is the
        minimal zero-spacing;
  \item $\|R_{pz}\|_2$ is the spectral norm of the effective bridge operator
        derived from $K$;
  \item $r_{\mathrm{bridge}}$ is the effective bridge rank as measured by
        singular values above a fixed relative threshold;
  \item $n_0$ is the number of zeta zeros used in the bridge;
  \item $(s_k)$ is the vector of nearest-neighbor spacings,
        $s_k = \gamma_{k+1}-\gamma_k$;
  \item $\tau_{\mathrm{gap}}$ is an optional scalar summarising the trend
        of the gap over time (e.g. a finite-difference estimate).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

In implementation, singular values $\sigma_0 \ge \cdots \ge \sigma_{m-1}$
of $K$ are computed via SVD of the cached kernel, and the bridge rank is
\[
  r_{\mathrm{bridge}} = \#\{j : \sigma_j > \varepsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}\,\sigma_0\}
\]
for some small relative threshold $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{rel}}$ (e.g.\ $10^{-6}$).

\section{Configuration-Sealed Certifiers (CSC)}

We now formalize the notion of a Configuration-Sealed Certifier (CSC),
motivated by the multiplicity-theoretic CRMF construction in genomics%
[file:10] and the Pro-tier Phase Mirror implementation.

\begin{definition}[CRMF-style CSC Configuration]
A CRMF-style configuration is a tuple
\[
  \mathcal{C} = (\mathrm{DomainTag}, P_N, n_0, \Theta),
\]
where:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
  \item $\mathrm{DomainTag}$ is a domain identifier
        (e.g.\ ``ecp-genomic'', ``phase-mirror-pro'');
  \item $P_N$ is a finite prime index set;
  \item $n_0$ is the number of modes/zeros;
  \item $\Theta$ denotes auxiliary hyperparameters (gain bounds, density tiers, etc.).
\end{itemize}
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Configuration-Sealed Certifier (CSC)]
A Configuration-Sealed Certifier is a certifier object $\mathsf{C}$ equipped
with a configuration $\mathcal{C}$ such that:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),leftmargin=*]
  \item $\mathsf{C}$ is constructed only via a factory
        $\mathsf{Factory} : \mathcal{C} \mapsto \mathsf{C}$ and cannot
        be reconfigured post-construction;
  \item compatibility between certifiers is defined structurally:
        \[
          \mathsf{Compatible}(\mathcal{C}_1,\mathcal{C}_2)
          \iff
          \mathrm{DomainTag}_1 = \mathrm{DomainTag}_2,\;
          P_N^{(1)} = P_N^{(2)},\;
          n_0^{(1)} = n_0^{(2)};
        \]
  \item certificates emitted by $\mathsf{C}$ carry the configuration
        fingerprint, so that consumers can verify configuration equality
        before accepting a certificate.
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

The configuration fingerprint can be implemented as a \emph{seal key},
e.g.
\[
  \mathrm{SealKey}(\mathcal{C})
  = \bigl(\mathrm{DomainTag}, P_N, n_0\bigr),
\]
or its hash. Two CSCs with different seals are non-interchangeable, even
if they share identical scalar input and output types.

\subsection{CSC in Phase Mirror Pro}

In the Phase Mirror Pro setting, the CSC configuration includes the
spectral constants:
\[
  \mathrm{DomainTag} = \text{``phase-mirror-pro''},\quad
  P_N = \{p_1,\dots,p_{n_p}\},\quad
  n_0 = N_0,
\]
and the shared spectral law parameters
\[
  \varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}} = 0.1,\quad
  N_0 = 64,
\]
which are used to define the Pro-tier gap floor
\[
  \Delta_{pz}^{\mathrm{floor}} = N_0^{-(1/2 + \varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}})}.
\]

\section{Pro-tier Certification and Two-Key Protocol}

We now describe the Pro-tier certification pipeline built on top of
SpectralWitness and CSC.

\subsection{FormalStabilityCertificate}

Let $\lambda_m$ denote the ACE-related contraction constant for the
pipeline and $\rho_t$ a computed contraction bound. A Pro-tier certificate
is:

\begin{definition}[FormalStabilityCertificate]
A FormalStabilityCertificate is a tuple
\[
  \mathcal{S}
  = (\lambda_m, \rho_t, W_{\mathrm{spec}}, \mathrm{epoch}),
\]
where $W_{\mathrm{spec}}$ is a SpectralWitness.
\end{definition}

We define three predicates:
\begin{align*}
  \mathrm{L0Satisfied}(\mathcal{S})
  &:= \rho_t < 1,\\
  \mathrm{SpectralHealthy}(\mathcal{S})
  &:= \delta_{pz} \;\ge\; n_0^{-(1/2+\varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}})},\\
  \mathrm{ProCertified}(\mathcal{S})
  &:= \mathrm{L0Satisfied}(\mathcal{S})
      \wedge \mathrm{SpectralHealthy}(\mathcal{S}).
\end{align*}

\subsection{UnifiedProWitness and Veto Decision}

A UnifiedProWitness binds mathematical and governance keys into a single
object that drives the oracle veto.

\begin{definition}[UnifiedProWitness]
Let $\mathsf{Compliance}$ denote a governance/compliance report and
$\mathcal{S}$ a FormalStabilityCertificate. A UnifiedProWitness is
\[
  \mathcal{W} = (\mathsf{Compliance}, \mathcal{S}),
\]
with predicates:
\begin{align*}
  \mathrm{GovernancePasses}(\mathcal{W})
  &:= \mathsf{Compliance.verdict} = \text{COMPLIANT},\\
  \mathrm{MathematicsPasses}(\mathcal{W})
  &:= \mathrm{ProCertified}(\mathcal{S}),\\
  \mathrm{BothKeysPass}(\mathcal{W})
  &:= \mathrm{GovernancePasses}(\mathcal{W}) \wedge
      \mathrm{MathematicsPasses}(\mathcal{W}).
\end{align*}
\end{definition}

The oracle veto outputs a VetoDecision:
\[
  \mathrm{VetoDecision}(\mathcal{W}, t)
  =
  \begin{cases}
    (\mathrm{vetoed} = 0,\ \mathrm{reason} = \varnothing,\ \mathrm{epoch} = t),
      & \text{if BothKeysPass},\\
    (\mathrm{vetoed} = 1,\ \mathrm{reason} = r,\ \mathrm{epoch} = t),
      & \text{otherwise},
  \end{cases}
\]
with reasons partitioned as
\[
  r \in \{\text{GOVERNANCE\_FAIL},\ \text{MATH\_L0\_FAIL},\ \text{SPECTRAL\_FAIL}\}.
\]

\section{Tiered Density and Tier 4 Analog}

CRMF introduces a tiered density scheme $(L_0,L_1,L_2,L_4)$ in which
Tier~4 computes a hypergraph spectral radius over operator compositions
when cheaper bounds fail.%
[file:10]

In Phase Mirror Pro, we define a homologous but distinct Tier~4 that
operates on the zeta-zero spacing distribution rather than operator word
norms.

\subsection{Phase Mirror Pro Tier 4}

Let $s_k = \gamma_{k+1}-\gamma_k$ denote normalized nearest-neighbor
spacings for the truncated zero set. The GUE Wigner--Dyson distribution
for normalized spacings is
\[
  P_{\mathrm{WD}}(s) = \frac{\pi}{2}s \exp\Bigl(-\frac{\pi s^2}{4}\Bigr).
\]
Define its CDF $F_{\mathrm{WD}}(s)$ and consider the empirical spacing
distribution $F_{\mathrm{emp}}(s)$ built from $\{s_k\}$.

\begin{definition}[Tier 4 GUE Recovery Check]
Given a SpectralWitness $W$, define:
\begin{align*}
  \mathrm{KS}(W) &= \sup_{s}
    \bigl| F_{\mathrm{emp}}(s) - F_{\mathrm{WD}}(s) \bigr|,\\
  \mathrm{deficit}(W) &= \Delta_{pz}^{\mathrm{floor}} - \delta_{pz}.
\end{align*}
A Tier~4 GUE recovery check returns a conditional status:
\[
  \mathrm{status}(W)
  =
  \begin{cases}
    \text{CONDITIONAL}, & \text{if }\mathrm{KS}(W) < \kappa,\;
                           \mathrm{deficit}(W) < \alpha \Delta_{pz}^{\mathrm{floor}},\\
    \text{HARD\_VETO}, & \text{otherwise},
  \end{cases}
\]
for chosen thresholds $\kappa,\alpha \in (0,1)$.
\end{definition}

This Tier~4 mechanism provides a \emph{recovery} path when the primary
gap test fails but the spacing distribution remains GUE-like and the
gap deficit is modest, enabling conditional certificates instead of
hard freezes.

\section{Finite-Dimensional Operator Norm Bounds}

In this section we collect explicit Lipschitz and operator norm bounds
for the finite-dimensional MultiplicityCell and ZetaCell constructions.
We work in the Frobenius norm on matrix-valued states, and product norms
for dual-sector states. These results specialise and extend the bounds
summarised in the main article for use in the Pro-tier certification
pipeline, including the bridge kernel and spectral witness structure.%
[file:1][file:2]

\subsection{Prime-Sector State Space and Norms}

Let $n_p,n_f \in \mathbb{N}$ and define the finite-dimensional prime
sector
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{p}^{(N)}
  = \mathbb{R}^{n_p \times n_f}, \qquad
  \|\Psi\|_F^2
  := \sum_{i=1}^{n_p}\sum_{j=1}^{n_f} \Psi_{i,j}^2.
\]
We identify $\mathcal{H}_{p}^{(N)}$ with $\mathbb{R}^{d_p}$ via a
vectorisation map
\[
  \mathrm{vec}: \mathcal{H}_{p}^{(N)} \to \mathbb{R}^{d_p}, \quad
  d_p = n_p n_f,
\]
which is an isometry between $\|\cdot\|_F$ and the Euclidean norm
$\|\cdot\|_2$.%
[file:2]

\subsection{Zero-Sector State Space and Product Norms}

Similarly, let $n_z,n_g \in \mathbb{N}$ and define the zero sector
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{z}^{(M)}
  = \mathbb{R}^{n_z \times n_g}, \qquad
  \|\Chi\|_F^2
  := \sum_{k=1}^{n_z}\sum_{\ell=1}^{n_g} \Chi_{k,\ell}^2.
\]
We identify $\mathcal{H}_{z}^{(M)}$ with $\mathbb{R}^{d_z}$ via a
vectorisation map with $d_z = n_z n_g$.

The dual-sector state space is the product
\[
  \mathcal{H}_{N,M} = \mathcal{H}_{p}^{(N)} \times \mathcal{H}_{z}^{(M)},
\]
equipped with the product norm
\[
  \|(\Psi,\Chi)\|^2 = \|\Psi\|_F^2 + \|\Chi\|_F^2.
\]
The vectorisation of $(\Psi,\Chi)$ into $\mathbb{R}^{d_p+d_z}$ is again
an isometry.%
[file:1]

\section{Lipschitz Bounds for Prime and Zero Blocks}

We first derive explicit Lipschitz bounds for the prime block $A_p$ and
zero block $A_z$.

\subsection{Prime Block $A_p$}

The prime block acts as
\[
  A_p(\Psi) = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl(W_p \,\mathrm{vec}(\Psi)\bigr),
\]
where $W_p \in \mathbb{R}^{d_p \times d_p}$ is a linear map with
spectral norm bounded by $\|W_p\|_2 \le \alpha_p$, and a componentwise
nonlinearity $\sigma$ is applied.%
[file:1][file:2]

\begin{lemma}[Prime Block Lipschitz Constant]
Assume that $\sigma:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz and
$\|W_p\|_2 \le \alpha_p$. Then $A_p$ is Lipschitz on
$\mathcal{H}_{p}^{(N)}$ with respect to $\|\cdot\|_F$, with constant
\[
  L_{A_p} \le L_\sigma \, \alpha_p.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Let $\Psi,\Phi \in \mathcal{H}_{p}^{(N)}$, and set
$u = \mathrm{vec}(\Psi)$, $v = \mathrm{vec}(\Phi)$ in $\mathbb{R}^{d_p}$.
Then
\[
  A_p(\Psi) - A_p(\Phi)
  = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl( \sigma(W_p u) - \sigma(W_p v) \bigr).
\]
Since vectorisation and reshaping are isometries,
\[
  \|A_p(\Psi) - A_p(\Phi)\|_F
  = \|\sigma(W_p u) - \sigma(W_p v)\|_2.
\]
By $L_\sigma$-Lipschitz continuity of $\sigma$ (applied componentwise),
\[
  \|\sigma(W_p u) - \sigma(W_p v)\|_2
  \le L_\sigma \|W_p u - W_p v\|_2.
\]
Using the spectral norm bound,
\[
  \|W_p u - W_p v\|_2 \le \alpha_p \|u - v\|_2
  = \alpha_p \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F.
\]
Hence
\[
  \|A_p(\Psi) - A_p(\Phi)\|_F
  \le L_\sigma \alpha_p \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F,
\]
as claimed.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Zero Block $A_z$}

The zero block has the same structure:
\[
  A_z(\Chi) = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl(W_z \,\mathrm{vec}(\Chi)\bigr),
\]
with $\|W_z\|_2 \le \alpha_z$. The same reasoning yields:

\begin{lemma}[Zero Block Lipschitz Constant]
Under the same assumptions on $\sigma$, with $\|W_z\|_2 \le \alpha_z$,
\[
  L_{A_z} \le L_\sigma \, \alpha_z.
\]
\end{lemma}

\section{Bridge Operators and Kernel-Based Lipschitz Bounds}

We now derive Lipschitz bounds for the prime--zero bridge operators
$C_{pz}$ and $C_{zp}$ built from an explicit-formula-inspired kernel.%
[file:1]

\subsection{Bridge Kernel and Operators}

Let $(p_i)_{i=1}^{n_p}$ be truncated primes and
$(\gamma_k)_{k=1}^{n_0}$ truncated zeta-zero imaginery parts. The bridge
kernel is
\[
  K_{ik} = \cos(\gamma_k \log p_i), \quad 1 \le i \le n_p,\; 1 \le k \le n_0.
\]
The bridge operators act via learnable maps
\[
  C_{pz}(\Psi)_k
  = \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} K_{ik} \, R_{pz}(\Psi_i),\qquad
  C_{zp}(\Chi)_i
  = \sum_{k=1}^{n_0} K_{ik} \, R_{zp}(\Chi_k),
\]
where $R_{pz}:\mathbb{R}^{n_f}\to \mathbb{R}^{n_g}$ and
$R_{zp}:\mathbb{R}^{n_g}\to\mathbb{R}^{n_f}$ are linear maps with
operator norms bounded by $\|R_{pz}\|_2 \le \beta_{pz}$,
$\|R_{zp}\|_2 \le \beta_{zp}$.%

\begin{lemma}[Bridge Operator Lipschitz Constants]
Define
\[
  S_p := \max_{1\le k \le n_0} \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} |K_{ik}|, \quad
  S_z := \max_{1\le i \le n_p} \sum_{k=1}^{n_0} |K_{ik}|.
\]
Then the bridge operators satisfy:
\begin{align*}
  \|C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\|_F
  &\le \beta_{pz} \, S_p \, \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F,\\
  \|C_{zp}(\Chi) - C_{zp}(\Omega)\|_F
  &\le \beta_{zp} \, S_z \, \|\Chi - \Omega\|_F.
\end{align*}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We prove the bound for $C_{pz}$; the $C_{zp}$ case is analogous.

Write $\Psi,\Phi \in \mathcal{H}_p^{(N)}$ and set
\[
  \Delta_i := \Psi_i - \Phi_i \in \mathbb{R}^{n_f}.
\]
The $k$-th row of $C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)$ is
\[
  \bigl(C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\bigr)_k
  = \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} K_{ik}\, R_{pz}(\Delta_i).
\]
Using the triangle inequality and the linear bound on $R_{pz}$,
\begin{align*}
 \left\|\bigl(C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\bigr)_k\right\|_2
 &\le \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} |K_{ik}| \,\|R_{pz}(\Delta_i)\|_2\\
 &\le \beta_{pz} \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} |K_{ik}| \,\|\Delta_i\|_2.
\end{align*}
By Cauchy--Schwarz,
\[
  \sum_{i=1}^{n_p} |K_{ik}| \,\|\Delta_i\|_2
  \le \left(\sum_{i=1}^{n_p} K_{ik}^2\right)^{1/2}
       \left(\sum_{i=1}^{n_p} \|\Delta_i\|_2^2\right)^{1/2}
  = \| K_{\cdot,k}\|_2 \, \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F.
\]
Therefore
\[
  \left\|\bigl(C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\bigr)_k\right\|_2
  \le \beta_{pz} \,\|K_{\cdot,k}\|_2 \, \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F.
\]
Summing over $k$ and using $\|\cdot\|_F$ on $\mathcal{H}_z^{(M)}$,
\[
  \|C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\|_F^2
  = \sum_{k=1}^{n_0}
      \left\|\bigl(C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\bigr)_k\right\|_2^2
  \le \beta_{pz}^2
       \left(\sum_{k=1}^{n_0} \|K_{\cdot,k}\|_2^2\right)
       \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F^2.
\]
If we use $\|K\|_2$ or the row-sum bound $S_p$, we can summarise this as
\[
  \|C_{pz}(\Psi) - C_{pz}(\Phi)\|_F
  \le \beta_{pz} S_p \|\Psi - \Phi\|_F,
\]
with an appropriately defined $S_p$; similarly for $C_{zp}$.
\end{proof}

We collect these into a combined bridge Lipschitz constant $L_C$ by
defining
\[
  L_{C_{pz}} := \beta_{pz} S_p, \qquad
  L_{C_{zp}} := \beta_{zp} S_z,\qquad
  L_C := L_{C_{pz}} + L_{C_{zp}}.
\]

\section{Time-Sieve and Internal Block Bounds}

We next consider a time-sieve operator $B$ acting along the prime and/or
zero indices via depthwise convolutions or Fourier multipliers, and
an internal block $E_{\mathrm{int}}$ acting via a flattened linear map
followed by a nonlinearity.%

\subsection{Time-Sieve Block $B$}

Flatten the dual-sector state $(\Psi,\Chi)$ to $z \in \mathbb{R}^{d}$,
with $d = d_p + d_z$, and let $W_B$ denote the matrix representation of
$B$. Suppose $\|W_B\|_2 \le \alpha_B$.

\begin{lemma}[Time-Sieve Lipschitz Constant]
If $B(\Psi,\Chi)$ is implemented by a linear map with spectral norm
bounded by $\alpha_B$, then
\[
  \|B(\Psi,\Chi) - B(\Phi,\Omega)\|
  \le \alpha_B \|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Immediate from linearity and the spectral norm bound on $W_B$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Internal Block $E_{\mathrm{int}}$}

Let $W_E$ be a linear map on the flattened state $z$ with
$\|W_E\|_2 \le \alpha_E$ and $\sigma$ the same Lipschitz nonlinearity as
before. Then $E_{\mathrm{int}}$ is
\[
  E_{\mathrm{int}}((\Psi,\Chi),x)
  = \mathrm{reshape}\bigl(\sigma(W_E z + U_E x)\bigr),
\]
for some fixed input projection $U_E x$. As in the prime-only case,
we obtain:

\begin{lemma}[Internal Block Lipschitz Constant]
For fixed input $x$, and under the same assumptions on $\sigma$ and
$W_E$, the internal block satisfies
\[
  \|E_{\mathrm{int}}((\Psi,\Chi),x) -
    E_{\mathrm{int}}((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|
  \le L_\sigma \alpha_E \|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|.
\]
\end{lemma}

\section{Residual Update and ZetaCell Contraction}

We now combine the above bounds to obtain a global Lipschitz bound for
the residual update and the ZetaCell map, yielding an ACE-style
contraction criterion.%
[file:1][file:2]

\subsection{Residual Map $T_m$}

Define the residual map
\[
  T_m((\Psi,\Chi), x) = (\Psi,\Chi) + m\, U((\Psi,\Chi),x),
\]
where $U$ decomposes as
\[
  U = A_p + A_z + C_{pz} + C_{zp} + B + E_{\mathrm{int}}.
\]
Let $L_{A_p}, L_{A_z}, L_C, L_B, L_E$ denote the Lipschitz constants derived
above. Then for fixed input $x$,
\[
  \|U((\Psi,\Chi),x) - U((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|
  \le L_U \|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|,
\]
where
\[
  L_U := L_{A_p} + L_{A_z} + L_C + L_B + L_E.
\]

\begin{lemma}[Residual Lipschitz Bound]
For fixed input $x$, the residual map $T_m$ satisfies
\[
  \|T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x) - T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|
  \le \bigl(1 + m L_U\bigr)
       \|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By definition,
\[
  T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x) - T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x)
  = (\Psi - \Phi, \Chi - \Omega)
    + m \bigl(U((\Psi,\Chi),x) - U((\Phi,\Omega),x)\bigr).
\]
Taking norms and applying the triangle inequality,
\begin{align*}
 \|T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x) - T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|
 &\le \|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|
      + m \|U((\Psi,\Chi),x) - U((\Phi,\Omega),x)\| \\
 &\le \bigl(1 + m L_U\bigr) \|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|.
\end{align*}
\end{proof}

\subsection{Non-Expansive Projectors}

Assume that the constitutional projector $P_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ and the
ethical projector $P_E$ are non-expansive with respect to the product
norm:
\[
  \|P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(Y) - P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(Z)\| \le \|Y - Z\|,
  \quad
  \|P_E(Y) - P_E(Z)\| \le \|Y - Z\|
\]
for all $Y,Z \in \mathcal{H}_{N,M}$. This follows from the composition
of norm caps, sparsity operators, and entropy-based rescalings designed
to be firmly non-expansive or approximated as such.%
[file:1][file:2]

\subsection{ZetaCell Contraction Theorem}

The ZetaCell map is
\[
  F((\Psi,\Chi),x) =
  P_E\bigl(P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x))\bigr).
\]

\begin{theorem}[ZetaCell Contraction]
Let $L_U$ be as above. If
\[
  1 + m L_U < 1,
\]
then for each fixed input $x$, the ZetaCell map $F(\cdot,x)$ is a
contraction on $\mathcal{H}_{N,M}$ with Lipschitz constant
\[
  L_F := 1 + m L_U < 1.
\]
Consequently, if there exists a closed ball $B_R$ invariant under $F$,
then $F(\cdot,x)$ admits a unique fixed point in $B_R$, and iterates
$((\Psi_t,\Chi_t))$ converge to this fixed point at a geometric rate
for any initial state in $B_R$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
For any $(\Psi,\Chi),(\Phi,\Omega)$ and fixed $x$, we have by non-expansiveness
of $P_{\mathrm{CSL}}$ and $P_E$:
\begin{align*}
 \|F((\Psi,\Chi),x) - F((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|
 &= \|P_E\bigl(P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x))\bigr)
      - P_E\bigl(P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x))\bigr)\|\\
 &\le \|P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x))
        - P_{\mathrm{CSL}}(T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x))\|\\
 &\le \|T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x) - T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|.
\end{align*}
Applying the residual Lipschitz bound,
\[
  \|T_m((\Psi,\Chi),x) - T_m((\Phi,\Omega),x)\|
  \le (1 + m L_U)\|(\Psi,\Chi) - (\Phi,\Omega)\|.
\]
Hence $F(\cdot,x)$ has Lipschitz constant at most $1 + m L_U$. If
$1 + m L_U < 1$, then $F(\cdot,x)$ is a strict contraction. The
Banach fixed-point theorem then guarantees a unique fixed point in any
closed ball $B_R$ invariant under $F$, and geometric convergence of
iterates.%
[file:1][file:2]
\end{proof}

\section{Spectral Gap Floor and Pro-Tier Bounds}

We now collect the Pro-tier spectral gap bounds used in the Pro-tier
certifier, linking the finite-dimensional zero-spacing metric and the
Pro-tier floor.

\subsection{Zero Spacing and Floor Definition}

Let $\gamma_1 < \dots < \gamma_{n_0}$ be the zeta zero heights used in
the bridge, with spacings
\[
  s_k = \gamma_{k+1} - \gamma_k,\quad
  1 \le k \le n_0 - 1,
\]
and define the minimal spacing
\[
  \delta_{pz} := \min_{1 \le k < n_0} s_k.
\]
The Pro-tier spectral floor is defined by
\[
  \Delta_{pz}^{\mathrm{floor}}
  := n_0^{-(1/2 + \varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}})},
\]
with a fixed offset $\varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}} > 0$ (e.g.\
$\varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}} = 0.1$).%
[file:1]

\begin{definition}[Spectral Health Predicate]
Given a SpectralWitness $W$ with fields $(\delta_{pz}, n_0)$, the
spectral health predicate is
\[
  \mathrm{SpectralHealthy}(W)
  :=
  \bigl(\delta_{pz} \ge n_0^{-(1/2 + \varepsilon_{\mathrm{spectral}})}\bigr).
\]
\end{definition}

\subsection{Pro-Tier Contraction Certificate}

A Pro-tier contraction certificate combines the contraction bound
$\rho_t$ with the spectral gap predicate.

\begin{definition}[Pro-Tier Contraction Predicate]
Given a FormalStabilityCertificate
\[
  \mathcal{S} = (\lambda_m,\rho_t,W_{\mathrm{spec}},\mathrm{epoch}),
\]
we define
\begin{align*}
  \mathrm{L0Satisfied}(\mathcal{S})
  &:= \rho_t < 1,\\
  \mathrm{ProCertified}(\mathcal{S})
  &:= \mathrm{L0Satisfied}(\mathcal{S})
      \wedge \mathrm{SpectralHealthy}(W_{\mathrm{spec}}).
\end{align*}
\end{definition}

This combines an ACE-style contraction condition on the pipeline with a
minimal zero-spacing condition, ensuring that the prime--zero bridge is
both contractive (in the sense of $F$) and spectrally non-degenerate.

\section{Tier~4 GUE Spacing and Recovery Bounds}

Finally, we outline the Tier~4 GUE spacing mechanism that supplements
the primary spectral gap floor with a nearest-neighbor spacing test.

\subsection{Nearest-Neighbor Spacing Normalisation}

Given zero spacings $s_k$, we define normalised spacings
\[
  \tilde{s}_k = \frac{s_k}{\bar{s}},\qquad
  \bar{s} := \frac{1}{n_0-1}\sum_{k=1}^{n_0-1} s_k,
\]
and consider the empirical distribution
\[
  F_{\mathrm{emp}}(x) = \frac{1}{n_0-1}
  \#\{k : \tilde{s}_k \le x\}.
\]

The GUE Wigner–Dyson nearest-neighbor spacing CDF is
\[
  F_{\mathrm{WD}}(x) = 1 - \exp\left(-\frac{\pi x^2}{4}\right).
\]

\subsection{Kolmogorov–Smirnov Statistic}

The Kolmogorov–Smirnov (KS) statistic for comparing $F_{\mathrm{emp}}$
and $F_{\mathrm{WD}}$ is
\[
  \mathrm{KS}
  := \sup_{x\ge 0} |F_{\mathrm{emp}}(x) - F_{\mathrm{WD}}(x)|.
\]

\subsection{Tier~4 Recovery Condition}

Given a SpectralWitness $W$ with $(\delta_{pz}, n_0)$ and spacings
$\{s_k\}$, and defining the gap deficit
\[
  \mathrm{deficit} = \Delta_{pz}^{\mathrm{floor}} - \delta_{pz},
\]
we introduce thresholds $\kappa,\alpha \in (0,1)$ and define:

\begin{definition}[Tier~4 Recovery Status]
The Tier~4 recovery status is
\[
  \mathrm{status}(W)
  =
  \begin{cases}
    \text{CONDITIONAL}, & \text{if }
      \mathrm{KS} < \kappa,\ \mathrm{deficit} < \alpha \Delta_{pz}^{\mathrm{floor}},\\[0.5ex]
    \text{HARD\_VETO}, & \text{otherwise}.
  \end{cases}
\]
\end{definition}

In particular, Tier~4 provides a \emph{conditional} certificate when the
primary gap falls below the floor but the spacing distribution remains
GUE-like and the deficit is modest, enabling a controlled form of
spectral recovery rather than an immediate and irreversible veto.
