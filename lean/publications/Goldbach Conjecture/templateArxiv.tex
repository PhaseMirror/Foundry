\begin{abstract}
We develop a framework based on Dynamic Recursive Meta-Mathematics (DRMM) and Certified Spectral Control (CSC) to study the Goldbach Conjecture. The proposal does not claim a proof, but establishes a verifiable and falsifiable pipeline for generating finite-window certificates. A key contribution is an explicit inequality linking spectral budgets to a uniform lower bound on Goldbach partitions. This recasts the problem into a convex optimization + certification paradigm.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The Goldbach Conjecture asserts that every even integer $2n \geq 4$ can be expressed as the sum of two primes. While unresolved, the problem lends itself to re-interpretation via recursive spectral operators. The DRMM/CSC framework provides a means to encode additive prime problems into lawful recursion with explicit budgets and certifiable outputs.

\section{Prime-Shift Operator Model}
Let $M \geq 4N$ and work on $\ell^2(\mathbb{Z}_M)$. For each prime $p$ define the shift operator
\begin{equation}
    (T_p f)(n) = f(n-p) \quad (\text{mod } M).
\end{equation}
The Bohr--prime channel is
\begin{equation}
    B_p(\omega) := e^{i \omega p} T_p.
\end{equation}
We have $\|B_p(\omega)\|=1$ and $\|\partial_\omega B_p(\omega)\| = p$, yielding explicit CSC channel bounds $b_p=1$, $b'_p=p$.

\section{Goldbach Autocorrelation}
For weights $w_p$ supported on primes $p\leq 2N$, define the weighted Goldbach count
\begin{equation}
    \widehat r_2(n;w) = \sum_{\substack{p\in\mathcal P \\ 2n-p\in\mathcal P}} w_p \, w_{2n-p} = (w*w)(2n).
\end{equation}
This is the autocorrelation of $w$ restricted to even indices.

\section{The Certification Inequality}
Let
\begin{align}
    r &= \sum_p |w_p|, \\
    s &= \sum_p p \, |w_p|, \\
    \mu &= \sum_p w_p^2 = \|w\|_2^2.
\end{align}
Then for all even $2n$ in the interior window we have the key inequality
\begin{equation}\label{eq:floor}
    \min_n \; \widehat r_2(n;w) \; \geq \; \mu - \frac{2}{\pi} r s.
\end{equation}
This bound follows from Fourier analysis: $\widehat r_2$ is the inverse transform of $g(\theta)=|\widehat w(\theta)|^2$, with $|g'(\theta)|\le 2rs$, hence bounded variation controls its Fourier coefficients.

\section{From Weighted to Literal Goldbach}
To obtain literal partitions, enforce $w_p \geq c > 0$ for all primes. Then
\begin{equation}
    r_2(n) \; \geq \; \frac{1}{c^2}\left(\mu - \frac{2}{\pi} r s \right).
\end{equation}
This ensures the certificate is in terms of actual Goldbach representations.

\section{Convex Certification Program}
We split the pipeline into design (convex optimization) and certification (evaluation of inequality~\ref{eq:floor}).

\subsection{Design Phase (LP)}
Fix budgets $(r, s_{\max}, \tau)$ and a support set $S_K$ of the $K$ smallest primes. Solve:
\begin{align*}
    \text{minimize } & \sum_{p\in S_K} p \, w_p \\
    \text{subject to } & \sum_{p\in S_K} w_p = r, \\
    & w_p \ge c, \\
    & \sum_{p\in S_K} |w_p - \kappa p^{-\alpha}| \le \tau \quad (\alpha \in \{0.7,1.0,1.3\}),
\end{align*}
with optional slope budget $\sum_{p\in S_K} p w_p \le s_{\max}$.

\subsection{Certification Phase}
After solving for $w$, compute
\begin{align}
    r &= \sum w_p, & s &= \sum p w_p, & \mu &\ge \frac{r^2}{K}.
\end{align}
The certified Goldbach floor is
\begin{equation}
    \varepsilon = \mu - \frac{2}{\pi}rs.
\end{equation}
If $\varepsilon>0$, then
\begin{equation}
    r_2(n) \ge \frac{\varepsilon}{c^2}, \quad \forall n \le N.
\end{equation}

\section{Scaling Strategy}
The key diagnostic is the behavior of $\varepsilon(N)$ as $N$ grows for fixed budgets. Possible regimes:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Collapse ($\varepsilon\to -$): failure of method.
    \item Plateau ($\varepsilon \to \varepsilon_0 >0$): strong evidence Goldbach holds.
    \item Growth ($\varepsilon \to +\infty$): potential path toward proof.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}
The method is lawful (budgets explicit), falsifiable (inequality must hold), and implementable with LP solvers. Even absent a full proof, the framework can push certified finite verifications far beyond brute force.
