\begin{abstract}
Prime encoding offers a unique framework for representing quantum information using discrete, non-overlapping prime numbers as fundamental units of computation. This paper explores the integration of prime encoding into the time-dependent and time-independent Schrödinger equation, highlighting its implications for quantum state representation, computation, and information transfer. We demonstrate how prime-based wavefunctions can serve as a discrete alternative to continuous eigenstate formulations, enabling applications in quantum cryptography, quantum error correction, and algorithm optimization.
\end{abstract}
